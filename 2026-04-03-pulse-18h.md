## Signal 1: Five Lives — Second French DTx Chase Alzheimer Reimbursement (After Ludocare)

One day after Ludocare's historic HAS reimbursement approval, a second French digital therapeutic is making moves. **Five Lives** (founded 2021, France + UK subsidiary) launched a crowdfunding campaign on Sowefund alongside an already-closed €1.7M raise to fund its brain-training platform for Alzheimer's patients.

**What Five Lives does:**
- Two apps: *Santé Cérébrale* (prevention, at-risk individuals) and *Five Lives Care* (therapeutic, launched January 2026 for diagnosed patients)
- Grounded in the **FINGER study** — demonstrated 50% reduction in dementia risk through multidomain intervention (cognitive stimulation + physical activity + nutrition + cardiovascular management + social engagement)
- 500 adapted physical activity videos, memory games, language exercises, sleep/nutrition coaching — positioned as "brain physiotherapy"
- CEO Xavier Louis's father was diagnosed 6 years ago; no curative treatment exists for the 60M patients worldwide (projected 150M by 2050)

**Why this matters for Alizé:**

Following Ludocare (asthma, pediatric), Five Lives is the second French DTx chasing reimbursement in a different indication. The pattern is clear: **France now has a functioning pathway for digital therapeutic reimbursement** when outcomes data is strong. For Alizé, this validates a healthcare vertical:

1. **Healthcare administrative automation** is adjacent to DTx reimbursement — as more digital health tools get reimbursed, healthcare administrative staff face more complexity (patient tracking, outcomes reporting, insurance coordination). AI agents that handle this coordination work are directly additive.

2. **FINGER-style multidomain approach is a model for Alizé's own positioning.** Five Lives doesn't sell a single feature — it sells a coordinated program. Alizé similarly doesn't sell "AI chatbots" — it sells coordinated agent workflows that span multiple systems and tasks. The positioning parallel is strong.

3. **Alzheimer's is the biggest demographic risk in France.** With an aging population and 1 in 3 people fearing Alzheimer's more than cancer, the market for brain health tools is enormous. Alizé could explore healthcare administrative agents for memory clinics, care coordination platforms, or insurance claim workflows in health.

## Signal 2: March 2026 Funding — €1.5B Raised, 74% AI, Late-Stage Is Back

Maddyness reported a blockbuster March for French tech: **€1.5B raised** across operations — +272% vs February (€407M) and well above the 2025 monthly average of ~€410M. Without AMI Labs (€890M), the month still comes in at €622M, which is a strong baseline.

**Key structural signals:**

- **Late-stage is back.** March had 3 late-stage rounds (vs 0 in March 2025). This is a meaningful signal: institutional capital is returning to growth-stage bets, not just seeds.
- **AI dominates even without AMI Labs.** AI companies raised €1.115B out of €1.5B total — 74%. Even stripping AMI Labs, AI still represents ~€225M of the non-AMI total, which tracks with the broader AI concentration trend.
- **Very few deals (8 operations this week vs 44 in March 2025).** The market is concentrating capital into fewer, larger tickets. This means competition for growth capital is fierce, but seed/Series A remains accessible.

| **Kestra** | €21.7M | Data/IA workflow orchestration | Open-source agent workflow platform — key Alizé infrastructure reference |

**Alizé takeaway:** Three new vertical AI SaaS companies (SCorp-io energy, AI6 insurance, Fairglow ESG beauty) all follow the same pattern: **domain-specific workflow + embedded AI + no-code configuration layer.** This is the product architecture that resonates with French investors and buyers right now. Alizé's offer — a managed AI agent layer for PME workflows — fits this template exactly.

## Signal 3: Vertical AI SaaS — Three New Entries This Week Confirm the Template

This week's new AI SaaS raises all follow the same pattern that Alizé should replicate:

**Pattern: Vertical Workflow + AI Agent + No-code Config → PME Pricing**

- **SCorp-io (€5M, energy):** AI + no-code energy management SaaS. Monitors and optimizes building energy consumption in real time. No-code configurator lets facility managers set rules without coding. B2B subscription model.
- **AI6 Technologies (€4M, insurance):** AI-driven insurance claims processing. Fast, data-driven, equitable claims assessment. Targets insurance companies (B2B2C). Strong data/proof narrative.
- **Fairglow (€3M, ESG beauty):** AI makes ESG reporting accessible and affordable for beauty industry players. Automates data collection and reporting. Targets beauty brands and suppliers.

**What these have in common with Alizé's intended model:**
1. Specific vertical domain (not generic)
2. AI embedded as core workflow engine (not a chatbot add-on)
3. No-code/low-code configuration for non-technical users
4. Outcome-oriented value proposition (energy savings, faster claims, compliance)
5. B2B subscription pricing

**Competitive note:** These are smaller raises but they confirm the market direction. Alizé should target the same product architecture for its first agent deployment.

## Signal 4: ManoMano Founders Exit — Vertical SaaS Warning, Not a Death Sentence

ManoMano's founders (Christian Raisson and Philippe de Chanville) are stepping down from operational roles after years of turbulence. The DIY/Home improvement marketplace raised €300M in 2021 at unicorn valuation, but has since:
- Cut 25% of workforce (230 jobs) in 2023
- Lost Next40/FT120 status
- Seen valuation drop significantly (per Mighty Nine study)

**Why this matters for Alizé:**

ManoMano is a cautionary tale for **scaling marketplace dynamics without durable unit economics.** But it's NOT an indictment of vertical SaaS — it's an indictment of venture-subsidized growth in a normalizing market.

For Alizé, the lessons are:
1. **Growth funded by capital rather than unit economics is fragile.** Alizé should build toward sustainable unit economics from day one, not assume a large raise will bridge the gap.
2. **The French vertical SaaS market still exists.** ManoMano failed partly because it tried to be Amazon for DIY in a market that couldn't support that model. A focused workflow automation agent for a specific PME type can be profitable without needing to be a platform.
3. **Loïc Derrien (new CEO) comes from industrial groups (Vinci, Hilti) — not tech.** This signals a shift from growth-at-all-costs to operational discipline. The next generation of French B2B companies will be judged on profitability and retention, not GMV.

## Signal 5: Maddyness "Future Leaders" Series — A More Grounded Generation of Founders

Maddyness published a series profiling 5 French entrepreneurs who are building with more lucidity than the "growth-at-all-costs" generation. Key themes:
- Personal origin stories driving business focus (not just "scaling for the exit")
- ARCAScience (AI for biomedical data, CEO had a brain tumor in 2014)
- Les Sherpas (accessible tutoring, CEO was a struggling student rescued by a tutor)
- Emphasis on operational sustainability and real customer value

**Why this matters for Alizé:**

This generational shift in founder mindset is a leading indicator for B2B buyer expectations. The next wave of French PME buyers will be founders who built their businesses conservatively — they will value agents that demonstrably save costs, not agents that promise transformational growth. Alizé's "outcome-based, measured, pragmatic" positioning is perfectly aligned with this cultural shift.

## Immediate Actions for Alizé

1. **Alizé should position as "vertical AI SaaS for PME" — not "AI agents."** The term "agent" is becoming generic. "AI workflow automation for [vertical]" is clearer and more defensible. Use the SCorp-io / AI6 / Fairglow positioning as a template: specific domain + AI core + no-code + measurable outcome.

2. **Healthcare vertical is validated by two reimbursement precedents in 48 hours.** If Alizé wants to differentiate from generic AI agent vendors, healthcare administrative automation is a strong vertical candidate. Start with a diagnostic: what administrative tasks in a French medical practice cause the most friction? (Patient follow-up, appointment scheduling, insurance coordination, reporting to HAS.)

3. **Kestra partnership should be explored.** Kestra just raised €21.7M as an open-source workflow orchestration platform. Alizé could use Kestra as the orchestration layer for its agents rather than building from scratch. This saves dev time and positions Alizé as a "Kestra-native" managed service provider.

4. **Domain decision is still the #1 blocker.** Every week without alize.ai (or equivalent) is a week without a real brand. Louis needs to decide today. Without a domain, every pitch starts with "we're a startup with no website yet."

5. **Build first LangGraph prototype this month.** The 14h pulse benchmarked LangGraph as the fastest/most efficient agent framework. Gabin should prototype a simple Alizé agent workflow (lead qualification or appointment scheduling) using LangGraph + Mistral AI as the model layer. This becomes the demo piece for first prospects.
