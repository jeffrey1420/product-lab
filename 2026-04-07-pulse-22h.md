## Signal 1: Anthropic's Infrastructure Stampede — 3.5 GW, $30B Run Rate, 1,000+ $1M Customers

Anthropic signed a new compute agreement with Google and Broadcom that dwarfs its previous commitments. A Broadcom SEC filing reveals the deal covers **3.5 gigawatts of compute capacity** — the largest single AI infrastructure commitment in history. The first gigawatt came online in 2025; the full 3.5 GW arrives in 2027, housed primarily in the U.S.

The business metrics are equally striking:
- **$30B revenue run rate** (up from $9B at end of 2025 — more than 3x in ~15 months)
- **1,000+ enterprise customers** spending $1M+ annually (doubled from 500 in under two months)
- **$380B valuation** after a $30B Series G

The U.S. Defense Department has labeled Anthropic a "supply-chain risk," which makes the infrastructure commitment even more notable — Anthropic is betting heavily that enterprise demand will remain sovereign-agnostic.

**What this means for Alizé:**
Claude's enterprise dominance is real and accelerating. The gap between AI leaders and everyone else is widening at the infrastructure level. For Alizé, this validates the strategy of building on top of proven frontier models rather than competing with them. The 1,000+ $1M customers are Anthropic's target; Alizé's target is the much larger universe of French PME spending €500-2,000/month who will never be Anthropic's direct customers but need managed Claude-powered agents.

## Signal 2: U.S. AI Labs vs. China — The Model Distillation War

In an unusual display of cross-company cooperation, OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google are sharing intelligence through the Frontier Model Forum to combat **adversarial model distillation** — Chinese AI labs systematically extracting capabilities from U.S. models to build cheaper clones.

Key details:
- Anthropic specifically blocked Chinese-controlled companies from Claude and identified **DeepSeek, Moonshot, and MiniMax** as the primary offenders
- OpenAI accused DeepSeek of trying to "free-ride on capabilities developed by OpenAI and other U.S. frontier labs"
- U.S. officials estimate unauthorized distillation costs Silicon Valley **billions annually** in lost profit
- The national security dimension: distilled models lack the safety guardrails U.S. labs invest heavily in

This is the AI equivalent of industrial espionage with extra steps. The Trump administration's AI Action Plan called for an information sharing and analysis center specifically to counter this threat.

**What this means for Alizé:**
For French PME, this creates an interesting dynamic. Chinese labs competing on price (open-weight, distilled models) create pricing pressure on U.S. labs, which respond by driving prices down for everyone. But French companies are increasingly sensitive to data sovereignty and compliance — which is exactly Alizé's positioning. "Powered by Claude, deployed in France, governed by us" is a stronger story when the alternative includes potentially ungoverned Chinese-distilled models.

## Signal 3: Anthropic's Life Sciences Push — $400M Coefficient Bio Acquisition

Anthropic acquired **Coefficient Bio** for approximately $400M — the company's most significant move into life sciences. Coefficient Bio was a New York-based startup focused on AI for biopharma. The acquisition builds on Anthropic's October 2025 launch of **Claude Life Sciences** — a vertical iteration of Claude designed for scientists, clinical trial coordinators, and regulatory managers.

Major pharmaceutical companies are already deploying Claude at scale:
- **Sanofi**, **Novo Nordisk**, and **AbbVie** are listed as key customers
- This follows Eli Lilly's $2.75B investment in Insilico Medicine's AI-driven drug design platform

**What this means for Alizé:**
Vertical AI agents work. Claude Life Sciences is proof that sector-specific AI agent deployments with proper domain fine-tuning create enterprise value. Alizé's approach — take Claude, connect it to PME tools via MCP, govern it, deliver it as a managed service — is the same model applied to a different vertical. Coefficient Bio validates the playbook.

## Signal 4: MCP Goes Mainstream — Medialister Opens Editorial Marketplace to AI Agents

Medialister (a PRNEWS company, Estonia-based, 72 employees) launched an **MCP server** allowing AI assistants (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) to interact directly with its editorial media marketplace. The workflow shift:

**Old:** brand → agency → email outreach → publisher (weeks, manual)
**New:** brand → AI agent → marketplace → publisher (minutes, automated)

Marketers can now ask their AI to find technology publishers matching specific criteria and budget constraints, negotiate terms, and manage campaigns — with humans stepping in only for strategy refinement. B2B tech, finance, and health-tech companies are the fastest-growing segment using this format.

**What this means for Alizé:**
This is a concrete example of MCP driving B2B service automation end-to-end. Medialister is to editorial placement what Alizé is to back-office AI — replacing fragmented, manual workflows with agent-mediated automation. The MCP protocol is becoming the connective tissue of the AI agent economy, and Alizé's MCP-first integration strategy is directly aligned with this trend.

## Signal 5: McKinsey on B2B Pricing in the Agentic AI Era (April 7, 2026)

McKinsey published a new piece today: **"B2B pricing: Navigating the next phase of the AI revolution."** The core argument: agentic AI promises a fundamental shift in how B2B pricing is set, managed, and optimized — creating both efficiency gains and pricing complexity.

Key implications for AI agent services:
- Traditional per-seat or per-user pricing models are being disrupted by usage-based and outcome-based models
- AI agents introduce real-time price optimization into B2B sales (agents negotiating with agents)
- Governance and trust become pricing variables — companies will pay a premium for accountable, auditable AI

**What this means for Alizé:**
Alizé's flat monthly retainer model (+ governance layer) is well-positioned. French PME don't want variable billing they can't predict. A fixed €800-1,500/month with clear scope, MCP connectors, and monthly reporting is exactly the pricing model that maps to the governance premium McKinsey identifies.
