## 1. Alize Target Customer Personas — First Draft

Based on Bpifrance data from prior pulses, synthesizing into formal buyer personas for Alize's managed IA service targeting French PME.

---

### Persona 1: "Le Dirigeant Opérationnel" — Primary Buyer

**Who:** CEO or General Manager of a service company, 30-55 years old, 20-100 employees.

**Company profile:**
- Services business (consulting, agency, professional services, B2B commerce)
- Existing SaaS stack (HubSpot, Slack, Notion, ERP-lite)
- No dedicated AI or data team
- Digitalization mostly done, but workflows still manual and siloed
- Revenue €1M-€15M

**Psychological profile:**
- Aware of AI but overwhelmed by options and vendors
- Primary motivation: operational efficiency (90% of PME per Bpifrance) — wants less waste, more output
- Secondary: commercial effectiveness — wants to do more with the same sales team
- Fears: losing control, looking stupid in front of employees, wasting money on hype
- Trusts peer recommendations over vendor pitch

**Decision process:**
1. Identifies a specific pain point (email overload, document processing, lead qualification)
2. Asks peers or Googles for solutions
3. Gets 2-3 vendor demos
4. Wants a "Quick Win" — proof of concept in under 6 weeks
5. Budget authority: €10K-30K for first engagement

**Quote to trigger:** "On a déjà essayé un outil d'IA mais ça n'a rien donné — c'était trop compliqué pour l'équipe."

**Quote that opens door:** "On s'occupe de tout, vous avez les résultats en 4 semaines."

**AI sophistication:** Low. Doesn't care about model size or architecture. Cares about output quality and not having to manage it.

---

### Persona 2: "Le DSI Occasionnel" — Technical Gatekeeper

**Who:** IT manager, office manager, or ops lead who wears multiple hats. 35-50 years old. May have some developer background or is the de-facto tech decision-maker in a small org.

**Company profile:**
- Manufacturing, logistics, or light industry with digital processes
- Existing tools: ERP, CRM, production software, Excel overload
- Dispersed data — some in cloud, some on premises
- No AI/data team; this person is the closest thing

**Psychological profile:**
- Concerned about data sovereignty — asks "where does the data go?" before anything else
- Frustrated by shadow IT and tools employees adopted without approval
- Wants to say yes to AI but needs a vendor he can trust with company data
- Values: security, compliance (EU AI Act), transparent pricing

**Decision process:**
1. Gets called in by CEO to evaluate a vendor
2. Checks data residency, model provider, compliance
3. Wants EU-based model or at minimum EU data processing agreement
4. Asks for references from similar companies
5. Becomes the internal champion if vendor passes muster

**Quote to trigger:** "Nos données, elles restent en Europe ?"

**Quote that opens door:** "100% sovereignty — modèle Mistral, données en France, contrat EU AI Act."

**AI sophistication:** Medium-low. Understands APIs and integrations but not LLM fine-tuning.

---

### Persona 3: "L'Intrapreneur数字化" — Internal Champion

**Who:** A department head, marketing manager, or operations lead, 28-45, who discovered AI tools personally and wants to bring it to their company.

**Company profile:**
- 50-200 employees, any sector but often services
- Has budget to spend (or can influence budget)
- Already using ChatGPT or similar personally
- Frustrated that company hasn't moved

**Psychological profile:**
- Excited about AI potential, reads about it on LinkedIn
- Needs internal credibility — wants to be seen as the one who "brought AI" to the company
- Will champion the project if they find a vendor they trust
- Risk-averse about career — won't push something that could fail publicly

**Decision process:**
1. Finds Alize (or is referred)
2. Builds internal business case with ROI estimate
3. Sells to CEO/manager
4. Becomes the day-to-day contact for implementation
5. Success here = internal AI champion for Alize post-deployment

**Quote to trigger:** "J'utilise ChatGPT tous les jours et je suis sûr qu'on pourrait automatiser ça en entreprise."

**Quote that opens door:** "On travaille avec votre équipe pour que ce soit eux qui déploient — et on forme vos champions."

**AI sophistication:** Medium. Knows what agents are, has played with GPTs or similar.

## 2. Quick Win Offer Design — First Draft

From Bpifrance: 60% of PME want Quick Wins (impact < 6 months, limited org change), budget €10K-50K.

Designing Alize's entry-level offer around this:

### Quick Win Offer: "1 Workflow, 4 Weeks, Preuve de ROI"

**What it is:**
- One specific, high-impact workflow automated with IA agents
- Delivered in 4 weeks: diagnostic (1 week) + deployment (2 weeks) + validation (1 week)
- Clear success metrics defined upfront
- You run it; we supervise and optimize

**Example workflows (ranked by ease of pitch to French PME):**

| # | Workflow | Pain it solves | Time to ROI | Difficulty |
|---|----------|---------------|-------------|------------|
| 1 | Email triaging + response drafting | Sales team overwhelmed by inquiries | 2-4 weeks | Easy |
| 2 | CRM data enrichment + lead qualification | Dirty CRM, sales time wasted on unqualified leads | 3-4 weeks | Medium |
| 3 | Contract/quote extraction + ERP entry | Manual data entry, errors, delays | 2-3 weeks | Easy |
| 4 | Internal knowledge base Q&A | Employees asking same questions repeatedly | 3-4 weeks | Medium |
| 5 | Meeting summary + task extraction | Post-meeting admin burden | 2 weeks | Easy |

**Pricing:** €10K-20K flat for Quick Win
**Expansion path:** +€5K-10K per additional workflow, or move to Monthly Retainer (€2K-5K/month)

## 3. Competitor Check: AI Agent Platforms Entering the PME Space

Checking whether enterprise AI agent platforms are making moves toward SMB/PME:

**Kestra** (enterprise focus, €36M raised, 25x growth) — targeting ops/IT teams, not PME decision-makers. Not a direct threat.

**ServiceNow AI Agents** — entering the enterprise but high complexity, high price. PME-adjacent but out of reach for €10K-30K budgets.

**Microsoft Copilot Agents** — making noise but still expensive and complex for sub-200 employee companies. Channel partners (ESN) are the delivery vehicle — Alize could position as a regional French alternative.

**French AI agents emerging:** Less noise than US players. The main gap in the French market is:
- No one speaking French SME language (not enterprise French, not startup French)
- No one doing "deploy and run" for €10K-30K
- No one addressing the data sovereignty concern head-on

**Conclusion:** Alize's window is open. The question is how fast they can build a repeatable Quick Win offer and get 2-3 reference clients.

## 5. Pulse Summary for Alize Product Direction

**What Alize should build next (priority order):**

1. **Email triage agent** — highest ROI, easiest pitch, aligns with Kuroba's own use case, French-language-ready
2. **CRM enrichment agent** — natural second workflow, connects to HubSpot/S Pipedrive
3. **Internal knowledge base Q&A** — differentiates Alize from commodity AI tools, showcases sovereignty

**What Alize should NOT build yet:**
- Complex multi-agent orchestration (overkill for PME Quick Wins)
- Industry-specific vertical solutions (too early, no reference clients)
- Open-source/self-hosted option (complexity, support burden)

**Product principle:** One workflow, deeply solved, at a time. Don't build the platform until you have 3 reference clients paying for 3 different workflows.
