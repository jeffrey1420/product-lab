## Signal 1: PE Is 80% of European Tech Deals Over €50M — Alizé's PE Pitch Just Got Stronger

**Source:** Maddyness + Clipperton, April 16, 2026

The structural shift from VC to PE in European tech is now quantified — and it's a direct Alizé tailwind.

Key data points:
- **80% of European tech deals over €50M now involve PE funds** (up from 8% in 2006-2010)
- "12 is the new 5": PE now requires 12% annual EBITDA growth for valuations that previously needed only 5%
- PE is actively arbitraging between VC (17% TRI target, high risk) and buy-out (13-14% TRI, lower risk) — favoring lower-risk operational plays
- US-specialized tech PE funds (PSG, Marlin) arrived in Europe 2019-2020, permanently restructuring the market
- SaaS maturity made tech companies "readable" to PE — they now understand recurring revenue and EBITDA conversion

**Why this matters for Alizé:**

1. **PE portfolio companies face AI governance mandates that didn't exist 18 months ago.** Post-Auchan and La Poste cyberattacks, PE firms managing €50M+ deals are acutely aware that AI agents are a new attack surface. AI governance is no longer optional — it's a portfolio risk management requirement.

2. **PE buys operational excellence, not growth narratives.** Alizé's pitch to PE is not "we'll grow your revenue" — it's "we'll make your AI operations defensible, auditable, and compliant while your competitors are exposed." That's an operational excellence story PE understands.

3. **PE firms can mandate Alizé across entire portfolios.** One PE fund mandating Alizé for all portfolio companies = 5-20 simultaneous AI governance deployments. This is the enterprise sales motion Alizé could never afford with individual outbound.

4. **PE exits require clean AI audit trails.** Due diligence for PE exits now includes AI system audits. Alizé's governance layer makes portfolio companies more exit-ready.
