## Signal 1: Agentova — A Direct Competitor to Alizé's Positioning (2,000 Users, France)

This is the most relevant new competitive signal for Alizé. **Agentova** (agentova.ai, headquartered in Cham, Switzerland) has surpassed 2,000 active users in France and is positioning itself as "the reference AI agent platform for entrepreneurs in France." Their pitch is nearly identical to Alizé's: replace fragmented automation tools with a unified, intelligent infrastructure where AI agents handle critical business functions.

**Key details:**

- "AI Brain" — contextual knowledge base with brand tone, products, processes, constraints
- No-code interface, connects to 3,000+ tools (Instagram, WhatsApp, Notion, LinkedIn, Gmail, etc.)
- Claims 10–20 hours saved per week per business, 82% productivity gain on average
- Client case: Celesty founder reduced monthly operational costs by 40,000 euros, team productivity doubled
- Client case: Mandyben Formation CEO: 35% revenue increase via automated prospecting and follow-up

- B2C/SME focused, no-code, self-serve — lower touch than Alizé's managed service model

**What this means for Alizé:**
Agentova is validating the market. French entrepreneurs are adopting AI agent platforms. But Agentova is self-serve/no-code — this is a SaaS product, not a managed service. Alizé's differentiation is the opposite: a high-touch, bespoke deployment + operation model for PME/ETI that includes governance, compliance, and ongoing supervision. Alizé should monitor Agentova's pricing, their enterprise motion, and whether they move upmarket toward larger clients. The 2,000-user milestone confirms demand exists; the question is whether Agentova's self-serve model can capture the PME segment or if they stay at micro-SME level.

## Signal 3: MCP (Model Context Protocol) 2026 Roadmap — Enterprise Readiness Is Now a Priority

The MCP official blog published its 2026 roadmap. Key highlights:

**Four priority areas:**
1. **Transport scalability** — Streamable HTTP at scale has gaps: load balancer conflicts, no standard session handling, no metadata discovery format. Fixes in progress. This is the foundation for remote MCP servers at production scale.
2. **Agent communication** — Tasks primitive (SEP-1686) shipped experimentally; production use surfaced retry semantics and expiry policy gaps. Next iteration coming.
3. **Governance maturation** — Moving from core-maintainer bottleneck to a delegation model where Working Groups accept SEPs in their domain. Open governance expanding.
4. **Enterprise readiness** — The newest priority. Enterprises deploying MCP hit predictable walls: audit trails, SSO-integrated auth, gateway behavior, configuration portability. No dedicated Enterprise WG exists yet — this is an open call for participation.

**Additional ecosystem data:**
- OpenAI announced MCP support across all products including ChatGPT desktop app
- Google launched Agent2Agent (A2A) protocol as MCP complement
- Over 1,000 community-built MCP servers now exist
- Lucidworks launched an MCP server (April 8, 2026) claiming to reduce AI agent integration timelines by up to 10x

**What this means for Alizé:**
MCP reaching enterprise readiness is a bellwether. When the standard protocol for connecting AI agents to enterprise data sources has audit trails, SSO auth, and gateway standardization, the managed service layer on top becomes more valuable, not less. Alizé should build MCP-native integrations into its deployment templates — being "MCP-ready" is a sales argument for enterprise IT teams that care about standards compliance. Also: the fragmentation between MCP and Google's A2A is worth watching. Two competing protocols means integration complexity for enterprises — Alizé can abstract this away.

## Signal 5: Pigment — French AI-Native SaaS Approaching $100M ARR, Adding "Modeler Agent"

Pigment (French AI-native enterprise planning platform, co-founded by Eléonore Crespo) is approaching $100M ARR, having tripled ARR for three consecutive years. Customers include Uber, Unilever, Anthropic, Siemens. 57% of new revenue from enterprise customers; 74% growth in enterprise customer base. 115% year-over-year increase in customers migrating from legacy SaaS (Workday, Oracle, SAP).

**The key move:** Pigment's "Modeler Agent" — users describe what they want in natural language and the agent generates governed, production-ready applications on Pigment's data engine. Figma reported reaching "80% of what they wanted to build from a blank page in minutes."

**Eléonore Crespo** advises President Macron on AI and speaks frequently about "AI sovereignty" but resists framing Pigment as a European champion vs. US companies. "I do not consider ourselves a French company. I consider ourselves a global company."

**What this means for Alizé:**
Pigment's success validates the French AI-native SaaS story. Their "Modeler Agent" is a direct analogy to what Alizé could build: a natural language interface that generates governed, production-ready agent workflows for specific business functions. Pigment is competing with SAP/Oracle/Workday in the enterprise planning space; Alizé is competing in a different vertical (operational IA agents for PME). But the template is the same: domain-specific, governed, AI-native, with measurable ROI. Pigment's ~$400M raised and $1B+ valuation also signals that French AI companies can reach significant scale without US dominance.

## Cross-Signal Synthesis: The French AI Market — April 9, 2026

1. **Alizé has a window, but it's closing.** Agentova (2,000 users, Swiss-French) and Nexus ($4.3M, Orange enterprise client) are validating and entering the market Alizé is targeting. Alizé needs to establish itself before larger players (Salesforce, SAP, or a well-funded US competitor) enter the French managed AI service space.

2. **The "deploy + operate + supervise" model is increasingly recognized as a distinct category.** Nexus raised funding on this model. Pigment's Modeler Agent points the same direction. Alizé's three-phase offer (diagnostic → pilot → managed service) maps to how enterprises actually buy AI.

3. **MCP enterprise readiness removes a friction point but also commoditizes basic integrations.** When any vendor can connect agents to enterprise data via MCP, the differentiator shifts from "can you connect" to "can you deliver outcomes." This favors Alizé's outcome-focused model.

4. **French regulatory moat is real and growing.** EU AI Act enforcement + Mistral's European infrastructure push + French government AI advisory (Crespo advising Macron) all create an environment where a French managed AI service with CNIL/AI Act expertise is more valuable than a generic US vendor.
