## This Cycle's Work: Anthropic Workflow Taxonomy Synthesis

**Source:** Anthropic Engineering — "Building Effective AI Agents" (anthropic.com/research/building-effective-agents, published April 2026)

**The taxonomy — what it actually says:**

Anthropic draws a clear architectural distinction:
- **Workflows:** LLMs and tools orchestrated through **predefined code paths**. Predictable, consistent, bounded. Right for well-defined tasks.
- **Agents:** LLMs that dynamically direct their own processes and tool usage. Higher capability, higher cost, higher risk. Right for open-ended problems.

**The six workflow patterns:**
1. **Prompt Chaining** — Sequential steps, each LLM processes previous output. Gate checks between steps. Example: invoice processing (extract → validate → categorize → route → archive).
2. **Routing** — Classify input, direct to specialized follow-up. Separation of concerns, specialized prompts. Example: customer ticket routing.
3. **Parallelization** — Sectioning (independent subtasks simultaneously) or Voting (same task multiple times for confidence). Example: contract reviewed by compliance LLM + financial LLM + legal LLM simultaneously.
4. **Orchestrator-Workers** — Central LLM dynamically breaks down tasks and delegates. Subtasks not predefined, determined at runtime. Example: research + report generation.
5. **Evaluator-Optimizer** — Generate → evaluate → refine loop. Example: content drafting with iterative brand voice review.
6. **Agents** — Full autonomy, dynamic tool usage, multi-turn operation. High cost, high risk. Requires sandboxing and checkpoints.

**Key insight:** "Most successful implementations weren't using complex frameworks. They were building with simple, composable patterns." — Anthropic

**What this means for Alizé's positioning:**

The taxonomy validates Alizé's managed service model. Most business AI use cases are workflows, not agents. Alizé designs bounded, governed AI workflows around specific business tasks. This is:
- More credible than "we deploy AI agents"
- More understandable to French PME buyers
- More governable under EU AI Act
- More measurable in ROI terms
