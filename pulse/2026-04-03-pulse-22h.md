## Signal 1: Kestra Raises €21.7M — The Enterprise Orchestration Race Is On

This is the most important competitive signal for Alizé this week. Kestra (France) raised a €21.7M Series A led by RTP Global, bringing total funding to €36M. The company originated from Leroy Merlin's internal tooling and is now an open-source workflow orchestration platform targeting enterprise.

**The numbers are striking:**
- 25× revenue growth in 18 months
- 2B+ workflows executed in 2025 (20× growth year-over-year)
- Client roster: JPMorgan, Toyota, Deutsche Telekom, Bloomberg, Crédit Agricole
- 50 → 100 employees planned (doubling)
- Cloud managed offering + Kestra 2.0 (distributed execution engine) in development

**What Kestra does that matters to Alizé:**
Kestra positions itself as "orchestration for data, IA, infrastructure and business operations." Their CEO explicitly says: "L'orchestration devient un sujet central, car il faut être capable de piloter des enchaînements de plus en plus complexes entre données, infrastructures et modèles." This is *exactly* Alizé's pitch — but Kestra is going enterprise top-down, and Alizé should go SMB bottom-up.

**The competitive differentiation map:**

| Dimension | Kestra | Alizé |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Target | Enterprise (Fortune 500) | SMB/PME French market |
| Model | Open source + cloud SaaS | Managed service (outcome-based) |
| Focus | Data/infra orchestration | Business task automation |
| Go-to-market | Sales-led, direct | Potential self-serve + cold outreach |
| Geography | Global (US/Europe/Asia) | France-first |

**Strategic implication for Alizé:**
Kestra proves the orchestration market is real and growing. But Kestra is solving a developer/DevOps problem. Alizé's angle should be *operational* — not "we orchestrate your data pipelines" but "our agents do your repetitive business tasks so your team doesn't have to." This is the same market category, different entry point.

The risk: if Kestra or a similar player (Airflow, Prefect, Temporal) decides to go downmarket into SMB, they could crowd out Alizé. Alizé needs to establish beachhead in French SMB before that happens.

## Competitor Deep Dive: What Alizé Should Learn from Kestra

Kestra's success validates several Alizé hypotheses:

1. **"Orchestration" is a credible, fundable category.** Investors understand it, enterprises are buying it. Alizé benefits from this narrative.

2. **Open source as a distribution strategy works.** Kestra got to 2B workflows by letting developers试用 it freely. Alizé could consider a free tier or open-source agent templates as a developer acquisition channel.

3. **The enterprise sales cycle is long but the contracts are large.** Kestra needed 18 months to get to Series A with enterprise traction. Alizé, targeting SMB, should expect faster sales cycles but smaller tickets. This suggests a product-led growth motion rather than sales-led.

4. **25× growth requires product-market fit + compounding usage.** Alizé's agents need to become embedded in client workflows so they're sticky. One-shot automation isn't enough — agents need to handle recurring tasks on a schedule.

**The Alizé counter-positioning:**
Kestra says: "orchestrate your data pipelines"
Alizé should say: "our agents do your recurring business tasks"

Same underlying technology, different buyer and job-to-be-done.
