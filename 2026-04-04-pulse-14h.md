## Signal 4: The Conversation — Agentic AI Academic Context: The Gap Between Hype and Reality

A March 26, 2026 academic article (The Conversation, citing Gartner, ACM, IEEE) provides important nuance:

**What agents actually do today**: Analyze requests, plan operation sequences, use external tools, evaluate results, adjust strategy. They don't just generate text — they act.

**The historical lineage**: Multi-agent systems research dates to the 1980s (Contract Net Protocol, HEARSAY-II). What changed is LLMs providing natural language interface and "statistical common sense" — making agents deployable by non-researchers.

**Critical risks**: Hallucinations + bias still exist in agentic systems. But the stakes change when agents can execute system commands, manipulate files, send network requests. Governance isn't optional — it's existential.

**The "supervision vs. execution" redistribution**: Agents don't replace humans; they shift the boundary between supervision and execution. This is the labor transformation argument — and it's where Alizé's "IA utile, sécurisée et suivie" message lands precisely.

**Alizé implication**: The academic framing of "agentique" as a historical evolution, not a revolution, supports Alizé's measured, pragmatic positioning. Not "AI will replace your team" but "your team gets a capable agent that reports back."
