## Signal 2: French Tech Funding Slowdown — €67M This Week (vs €107M Prior Week)

Maddyness weekly roundup (April 10): French startups raised €67M across 7 operations, with average ticket of €9.5M. This is a significant drop from the prior week's €107M.

**AI funding specifically:** Only 2 AI startups raised this week — Cobl.ai (€6M) and Omniscient (€3.5M). Total AI raised: €9.5M.

**Context:** March 2026 was a strong month (€1.5B, AI at 74%). But the weekly data is volatile. The trend matters more than any single week.

**Cobl.ai — most interesting new AI company this week:**
- **What it does:** Multi-agent AI platform for automating commercial document creation (proposals, bids, presentations)
- **Founded:** 2020, raised €1M in 2023, now €6M in 2026
- **Backers:** Eurazeo (major French VC), Station F

**What this means for Alize:**

Cobl.ai is the most directly relevant new company. Its agent architecture (orchestrator + specialized agents + human checkpoints) is identical to the Alize governance model. The fact that:
1. Eurazeo (serious French VC) backed it
2. Enterprise clients like Orano and Econocom are paying
3. The CEO explicitly talks about agents that "question and challenge" rather than just executing

... validates the Alize positioning. "Agents that work with humans, not instead of them" is the winning formula in French enterprise.

Also notable: Cobl.ai connects to Notion, Teams, Salesforce, Gmail — showing that multi-tool integration is the baseline expectation for enterprise AI agents in 2026. Alize's tool sandboxing approach is on the right track.

## Signal 3: French Fintech100 — 57% EBITDA+, 46% Planning M&A

The 5th annual Fintech100 ranking (Finance Innovation + Truffle Capital + Groupe BPCE + Sopra Steria) reveals a maturing French fintech sector:

**Key numbers:**
- 57% of top 100 French fintechs are now EBITDA positive
- 37% of companies created post-Covid (within 5 years) are already profitable
- 75% of revenue is recurring (more than 50% of total revenue)
- 46% planning M&A or consolidation
- 62% of companies considering sale prefer large group acquirer
- 73% already present or planning international expansion
- Average funding round down; bank financing up 20 points YoY

**Implications for Alize:**

The fintech sector is Alize's ideal beachhead vertical. Why:
1. **57% profitability = cash to invest in AI**: EBITDA-positive fintechs have budget for managed AI services
2. **75% recurring revenue = stable operations = AI deploymentfriendly**: Predictable cash flows mean AI operational costs are manageable
3. **46% M&A plans = organizational chaos**: Companies in acquisition mode have fragmented systems and manual processes — exactly where AI agents add most value
4. **Qonto dominates** (top of Fintech100), serving TPE/PME: Alize should study Qonto's customer base as a potential TAM

**Fintech as Alize vertical thesis:**
French fintechs handle大量 repetitive back-office work (reconciliation, compliance, reporting, customer onboarding). They are early AI adopters, have modern tech stacks, and their investors push for efficiency gains. Alize's managed agent service for fintech back-office is a strong product concept.

## Deep Dive: Cobl.ai — The French Multi-Agent Model Alize Should Study

Cobl.ai (formerly Thinkeo, pivoted 2023→2026) is the most architecturally similar company to Alize in the French AI landscape right now. Here's why:

**The Cobl.ai model:**
1. Orchestrator agent understands context of the sales rep's task
2. Specialized sub-agents handle layout, compliance, visuals, QC
3. Agents question and challenge the human throughout — not just execute
4. Human has final say on every document
5. Integrates with Notion, Teams, Salesforce, Gmail

**This is exactly the Alize governance model applied to sales documents.** The difference: Alize applies it to all back-office workflows, not just sales proposals.

**What Alize can learn from Cobl:**
- Their positioning is "the agent that makes you think harder" — not "the agent that replaces you." Alize should use the same framing.
- Their enterprise clients (Orano, Free, Econocom) are exactly the type of French companies Alize should target.
- The fact that Eurazeo invested validates that French VCs understand and back the "AI that works with humans" model.
