## 1. French PME AI Buyer Behavior: Bpifrance Study of 1,200 Companies

**Source:** Bpifrance Conseil — "IA dans les PME: de l'intention a l'action", based on 1,200 company survey + 700 IA Booster consulting missions (2024 data)

This is the most detailed French SME AI buyer behavior dataset available. Critical for Alize's go-to-market.

### What French PME Want from AI (top motivations)

| Priority | Goal | % of companies |
|----------|------|----------------|
| 1 | Operational efficiency / process optimization | 90% |
| 2 | Customer relations / commercial effectiveness | 65% |
| 3 | Product innovation / customer experience transformation | 64% |
| 4 | Business model transformation | 30% |

**Key insight:** French PME are primarily motivated by **operational efficiency** (90%), not AI sophistication. This validates Alize's positioning: the pitch is not "AI" — it's "less waste, more output."

### Why PME Haven't Adopted Yet (the barriers)

When examining PME that are still on the sidelines:
- **88% cite lack of internal expertise/skills** — the #1 blocker
- **~33% have no data strategy** — data is siloed or not digitized
- Cost concerns and fear of misuse are secondary
- Immature "plug and play" AI solutions for SME needs

**Implication for Alize:** The expertise gap (88%) is the entry point. Alize doesn't just sell an AI agent — it sells the expertise to deploy, manage, and supervise it. The managed service model (deploy + operate + supervise) directly addresses this barrier.

### The Quick-Win Bias

French PME strongly prefer **"Quick Win" projects**:
- **60%** of prioritized use cases are Quick Wins (impact < 6 months, limited org change)
- **30%** are "Critical" projects (medium-term transformation, €50K-100K budget)
- **10%** are "Disruptive" projects (long-term, €100K+, business model change)

**Budget ranges:**
- Quick Win: €10,000–€50,000
- Critical: €50,000–€100,000
- Disruptive: €100,000+

**Implication for Alize:** Alize's entry offer should target the Quick Win category — €10K-50K range, < 6 months, clear operational gain. Email workflow automation is a perfect Quick Win use case: tangible, limited org change, fast to demonstrate ROI.

### Use Case Distribution

- **61%** use generative AI (chatbots, automated reports)
- **24%** use predictive analytics
- **18%** use constraint optimization / operations research
- **9%** use computer vision (anomaly detection)

## 2. The French AI Adoption Framework: 7 Levers for Success

**Source:** Bpifrance (same study)

These are what French PME identify as success factors for AI adoption:

1. **Data quality as the foundation** — digitalize first, then AI
2. **Employee acculturation** — demystify AI, get buy-in
3. **Co-creation with teams** — people adopt what they help build
4. **Leadership sponsorship** — CEO + DSI alignment required
5. **Resource and budget planning** — POC + industrialization both cost money
6. **Internal AI champion** — a dedicated internal referent post-deployment
7. **Data sovereignty** — strategic thinking on where data lives and who accesses it

**Implication for Alize:** Levers 1, 6, and 7 are Alize's differentiation. Most managed service providers will ignore 7 (sovereignty). Alize should make it a pillar — French law, EU AI Act compliance, Mistral or EU-based models.

## 3. Data Sovereignty as a Buying Criterion

**Source:** Bpifrance (same study)

The study explicitly calls out that **souverainete et securite des donnees** (data sovereignty and security) is a strategic consideration for PME when adopting AI. French business owners are increasingly asking:
- Where does my data go?
- Who can access it?
- Am I compliant with EU AI Act?

This is a **growing** concern, not a static one. As US hyperscalers push harder into the French market, PME decision-makers will face more scrutiny from their own IT teams and potentially regulators.

**Implication for Alize:** Position Alize as the French/EU-sovereign alternative to US AI agents. Not "French AI" as a marketing slogan — but concrete: EU AI Act compliance, data residency in EU, transparent model choices (Mistral as default).

## 4. Bpifrance "AI Booster" — The Subsidy Funnel

**Source:** Bpifrance

Bpifrance runs an **"AI Booster"** program as part of the national "Osez l'IA" plan, which funded 700 AI consulting missions in 2024. This means:
- French PME can get **subsidized diagnostic** of their AI readiness (free or low-cost)
- The government is actively pushing AI adoption through subsidies
- This creates a pipeline of PME that have been **pre-diagnosed** and are ready for implementation

**Implication for Alize:** These 700 companies (and growing) represent warm leads. They've already paid someone to tell them they need AI. They just need someone to deploy and run it. Alize should position as the "post-diagnostic implementation partner."

## 5. Alize Buyer Persona: Synthesis from Bpifrance Data

Based on the Bpifrance data, the Alize ideal customer profile:

**Profile:** French PME, 20-200 employees, services or light manufacturing, existing digital stack (some SaaS tools), no dedicated AI/Data team, CEO or operational manager driving the decision.

**Decision drivers:**
- Want quick operational wins, not AI sophistication

**Typical objection:** "We don't have the internal skills to manage this"
**Alize answer:** "That's exactly what we handle. You get the output, we manage the AI."

**Budget authority:** €10K-50K for first engagement (Quick Win), with expansion path to €50K-100K
