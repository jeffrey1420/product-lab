## Signal 1: Deloitte — SaaS Meets AI Agents (2026 Predictions)

Deloitte's "TMT Predictions 2026" has a substantive piece on how enterprise SaaS platforms are incorporating AI agents. Key findings:

**The Agentic SaaS Transformation:**
- SaaS applications in 2026 are evolving from passive tools to active collaborators — intelligent, personalized, adaptive, and increasingly autonomous
- The shift is from "software that does X" to "software that achieves outcome Y with minimal human intervention"
- Multi-agent orchestration within SaaS platforms is the next frontier — agents from different vendors coordinating across systems

**Pricing Model Revolution:**
- Traditional per-seat/per-subscription pricing is being challenged by usage-based, value-based, and outcome-based models
- Examples: pricing tied to "tickets resolved by AI agent" or "employees hired via HR agent"
- This creates revenue unpredictability for vendors but also new alignment opportunities
- Key challenge: defining what "an agent," "a task," "a process," and "an outcome" actually mean — requires vendor-customer contract negotiations

**The "Attention Layer" Opportunity:**
- Where do humans interact with AI agents? Deloitte predicts competition around the "interaction layer" — standalone SaaS apps, management platforms, or third-party "control centers"
- Agent marketplaces (internal + external) where businesses discover and integrate new agent capabilities dynamically
- This is directly relevant to Alizé: positioning as the "attention layer" / governance control plane for French PME is validated by this trend

**UI/UX as Differentiator:**
- Three dimensions of AI agent interfaces in SaaS: personalized/proactive, conversational (natural language replacing menus), and diagnostic (explainability + auditability)
- Diagnostic capability is critical for enterprise trust — users must be able to reconstruct agent decision-making

**Alizé implication:** Deloitte's framing validates Alizé's managed service positioning. The "attention layer" / "control center" is exactly what Alizé can offer French PME — a trusted interface between AI agents and business operators. The pricing model revolution (outcome-based) is also Alizé's natural territory: "we deploy agents that produce X outcome, monthly retainer."
