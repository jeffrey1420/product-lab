## Signal 1: Fintech100 2026 — 57% EBITDA+, 46% Planning M&A

The Fintech100 ranking (Finance Innovation + Truffle Capital, with Groupe BPCE and Sopra Steria) was published this week. This is the most important French fintech dataset of the year. Key findings:

**Profitability breakthrough:**
- 57% of the 100 largest French fintechs now EBITDA positive (up significantly from prior year)
- Among companies less than 5 years old (post-Covid): 37% already profitable — the path to profitability is compressing
- 75% of companies have recurring revenue representing >50% of total revenue
- Total combined revenue: €3.5B (+36% YoY)

**M&A pipeline is massive:**
- 46% of fintechs are planning a growth acquisition or merger
- 35% are considering a sale
- Of those considering a sale: 62% prefer a large group as buyer, only 8% prefer another fintech
- "It will be international actors who want to acquire French companies" — Bernard-Louis Roques, Truffle Capital

**International expansion:**
- 73% are already present or planning to expand internationally
- 67% targeting Europe, driven by regulatory harmonization (DORA, PSD3)

**AI is transforming business models, not just operations:**
- 56% of fintechs report AI is changing their core business model (2x vs 2023)
- Key question being asked: "What am I actually selling? Data? Personalization? New pricing models?"
- This is the SaaS transition moment for AI — the shift from "AI inside" to "AI as the product"

**Alize angle:**
The Fintech100 data confirms three things for Alize:
1. **French fintechs are Alize's ideal customer profile:** profitable, recurring revenue, complex back-office ops, planning M&A (which creates massive operational workload)
2. **M&A pipeline = immediate agent deployment opportunity:** when companies merge, their back-office processes (HR, finance, legal, compliance) must be integrated rapidly. AI agents that can accelerate integration are directly valuable.
3. **The "AI business model shift" framing validates Alize's positioning:** Alize doesn't sell AI tools, it sells operational outcomes that happen to be delivered by AI agents. The Fintech100 data confirms that the question "what are we actually selling?" is top of mind for French fintech leadership.

## Signal 2: WeMaintain Acquired by Otis (~€300M)

French maintenance-tech startup WeMaintain (elevator/escalator installation and maintenance) was acquired by American giant Otis Worldwide. Key details:

- Transaction: ~€300M, 80% stake, cash
- Founded 2017 by Jade Francine and Benoît Dupont
- ARR: "several tens of millions" — not disclosed precisely
- Clients: BNP Paribas, SNCF, Accor
- Geography: France, UK (main market), Singapore, Hong Kong (fastest growth — won Hong Kong Metro maintenance contract)
- Employees: ~350 worldwide
- Raised: >€50M from Eurazeo, Bpifrance Digital Venture, Red River West, Financière Saint James
- Expected profitable this year
- Management retaining 20% and staying on

**Why this matters for Alize:**
- This is a pure B2B infrastructure services company (not fintech, not AI-native) that built recurring revenue through enterprise contracts
- The exit validates the "French B2B services + technology" model — companies that solve operational problems for large French corporates are acquisition targets
- WeMaintain's problem (managing maintenance operations for a distributed physical asset base) is analogous to what Alize agents could solve in other verticals: coordinating dispersed, repetitive, compliance-heavy operational workflows
- The €300M valuation on "tens of millions ARR" implies ~10-15x ARR — healthy multiple for operational services businesses
- Eurazeo (which backed Cobl.ai and WeMaintain) is emerging as a key investor for the AI agents in B2B operations thesis

## Signal 3: Neat + Visa — Embedded Insurance at 25M Cards Scale

French insurtech Neat signed a partnership with Visa to embed insurance solutions directly into payment cards. Details:

- Deployment: France first, then Europe
- Potential reach: 25 million French cardholders
- Could 4x Neat's customer base
- Founded 2022 by Fabien Cazes and Maximilien Dauzet
- Raised: €50M Series A in 2024
- Already deployed at: SNCF, Pierre et Vacances, Center Parcs, leboncoin, Cdiscount, Decathlon
- Present in 8 countries
- Uses AI for claims management and offer personalization

**Why this matters for Alize:**
- Neat's insurance embedded in cards is the same architectural pattern as Alize agents embedded in enterprise workflows: go where the work already happens
- The Visa distribution partnership is a "channel partnership" — Neat didn't build its own app, it deployed inside existing infrastructure
- Alize should think about its own "Visa equivalent" — which enterprise software platforms could carry Alize agent capabilities the same way?
- Neat's AI use case (claims analysis, personalization) is a concrete example of AI changing the business model, not just operations — relevant for Alize's fintech vertical pitch

## Signal 5: Belitsoft AI Agent Development Forecast 2026

Belitsoft published an AI agent development forecast for 2026 (April 8). Key data points:

- 40% of enterprise applications will include task-specific AI agents by end of 2026
- Enterprises are running an average of 12 AI agents, but roughly half operate in isolation without cross-agent coordination
- The shift is from single AI assistants to coordinated multi-agent systems
- Main barriers to wider adoption: integration complexity, security concerns, lack of clear governance frameworks
- Source: Futurum Group 1H 2026 Enterprise Software Decision Maker Survey

**Alize angle:**
The "12 agents but half work alone" finding is the multi-agent orchestration problem — exactly what Alize is positioned to solve. The governance and integration barriers are Alize's core value proposition. This data should be in Alize's sales deck.
