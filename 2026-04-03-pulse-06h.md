## Signal 1: March 2026 Funding Data — AI Now 74% of All French VC (€1.15B of €1.5B)

Maddyness published the full March 2026 funding roundup. The data is unambiguous: **AI has become the dominant category in French VC by an overwhelming margin.**

Key numbers:
- Total raised in March 2026: €1.512B (37 operations)
- AI startups: €1.115B across 15 companies = **74% of all capital raised**
- This is a dramatic jump from February 2026 (€107M in AI) and a complete outlier vs 2025 average (~€40M/month for IT & IA)
- AMI Labs alone: €890M (Yann LeCun's startup, 59% of total market)
- Second place: Pasqal (quantum computing) at €170M
- Alan: €100M (insurtech, now also AI-powered)
- Qevlar AI: €25.8M (agentic security)
- Waiv: €24.3M (AI pathology diagnostics, Owkin spin-out)
- Escape: €15.6M (AI security testing)

Regional concentration: 97% of capital went to Île-de-France (Paris), vs 59% in March 2025. Only 7 regions appeared in the dealflow.

**What this means for Alizé:**
1. **The window for AI positioning is now**: AI is no longer a niche — it's the market. French VCs are all-in. This creates both opportunity (AI credibility is high) and risk (every SaaS is now an "AI company").
2. **Late-stage is back**: 3 late-stage rounds in March 2026 vs 0 in March 2025. The market is maturing, which means Series A/B AI companies will start competing for PME market share soon.
3. **Geographic concentration is extreme**: No region outside Paris raised significant capital. Alizé's positioning in Caen/France is not a disadvantage — it's where the customers are, and they won't be flooded with AI agent pitches from well-funded Paris startups.
4. **Qevlar AI (€25.8M) is the most relevant comparable**: Agentic security AI, similar stage to where Alizé could be. Their raise validates the "agents for operational workflows" positioning.

## Signal 2: Omniscient — Multi-Agent Architecture for Reputation Intelligence

Omniscient (French decision intelligence startup) raised €4.1M in pre-seed from Seedcamp, Drysdale Ventures, Plug and Play, Raise, and Bpifrance. Founded by two former McKinsey consultants (Arnaud d'Estienne and Mehdi Benseghir). Client: Renault.

**What makes this relevant:**
- Their platform is explicitly built on **specialized AI agent architectures** — different agents for regulation, supply chain, competitive intelligence, each synthesizing data from their domain
- 100,000+ data sources (media, social, web, audio, video, internal channels)
- Real-time synthesis into executive dashboards
- The pitch: "detect weak signals before they become critical"

**The McKinsey pedigree angle**: The founders identified the same problem Alizé solves in other domains — organizations are drowning in data but can't translate it into timely decisions. The McKinsey background means this is a sophisticated, enterprise-focused sales motion.

**What this means for Alizé:**
Omniscient is deploying the exact same architectural pattern (multi-agent specialized systems + orchestration + human oversight) for a different vertical (reputation/crisis management). This validates the model. The question for Alizé is: which vertical in French PME has the most acute version of "drowning in repetitive tasks and dispersed tools"?

## Signal 3: Alan Reaches €100M — Insurtech Meets AI Agent

Alan hit €100M in March 2026, making it one of the largest raises of the month. This is significant because Alan has been progressively adding AI features to its health insurance platform — including AI-powered claims processing, proactive health recommendations, and recently, AI agent-like features for administrative tasks.

**What this means for Alizé:**
Alan is the clearest French example of a vertical SaaS company successfully layering AI onto an existing product. Their model (insurance + AI agents for administrative tasks) could be a template for Alizé's partner strategy — if Alizé can become the agent engine behind vertical SaaS platforms like Alan, the distribution is solved.

## Signal 4: Kestra Deep Dive — The Enterprise Orchestration Playbook

Kestra's $25M Series A article reveals more detail than the initial brief:

- Originally built internally at Leroy Merlin (France's largest home improvement retailer)
- Their pitch: "as AI agents proliferate, something has to coordinate workflows" — this is Kestra's value proposition, and it's exactly the problem Alizé's governance layer solves
- Their enterprise clients (JPMorgan, Toyota, Deutsche Telekom, Bloomberg, Crédit Agricole) confirm that orchestration is now a C-suite concern
- Kestra 2.0 coming: new distributed execution engine + managed cloud offering
- Open source core → enterprise monetization is the playbook

**The key quote**: "L'orchestration devient un sujet central, car il faut être capable de piloter des enchaînements de plus en plus complexes entre données, infrastructures et modèles."

**What this means for Alizé:**
Kestra and Alizé are playing on the same problem but at different layers and scale points. Kestra = enterprise infrastructure orchestration. Alizé = SME operational AI agents + governance. The natural extension for both: Kestra could add agentic capabilities; Alizé could add orchestration. The question is whether they become partners or competitors as the market matures.

## Immediate Actions for Alizé

1. **Domain name — still the #1 blocker**: Louis has been sitting on this for 3+ weeks. Every day without a domain is a day without a brand. alize.ai or alize.fr needs to be purchased today. This is blocking everything else.

2. **Qevlar AI partnership conversation**: Qevlar raised €25.8M for agentic security. They have Atos and Orange Cyberdefense in their client list. If Alizé positions itself as the agent deployment layer for French companies, Qevlar is a natural integration partner — they handle the security agents, Alizé handles the operational agents.

3. **Alan's model is the distribution playbook**: Don't try to sell direct to French PME. Alan-style: find vertical SaaS platforms that already have the customer relationships and layer Alizé agents into their workflow. Distribution before product-market fit.

4. **Omniscient's multi-agent architecture validates Alizé's technical direction**: The market is confirming that specialized AI agents + orchestration + human oversight is the right pattern. Alizé should lean into this architecture publicly.

5. **First pilot is still the priority**: The market validation is overwhelming. The question is no longer "is there a market for AI agents in French PME?" — the question is "can Alizé execute on the first deployment?" One real pilot changes everything: credibility, learning, case study, reference.
