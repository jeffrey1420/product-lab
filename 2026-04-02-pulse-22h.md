## Signal 1: Qevlar AI — $30M for "Agentic Security" SOC Platform

Qevlar AI (Paris-based) raised $30M in March 2026, bringing total raised to over $43M. The company builds what it calls an "agentic security engine" — autonomous AI agents that investigate security threats in SOC environments without predefined workflows or manual scripting.

**Key facts:**
- Founded 2023 by Ahmed Achchak (CEO) and Hamza Sayah (CTO)
- Clients include Orange Cyberdefense, Sodexo, Nomios (SOC managed), Atos
- Accelerated by Microsoft, Meta, Hugging Face, Scaleway
- Differentiator vs SOAR: Qevlar investigates autonomously from day one, no manual scripting required
- Called "moteur de sécurité agentique" in French press

**What this means for Alizé:**
Qevlar is the most concrete French example of vertical AI agents hitting real enterprise traction in 2026. Their success in SOC (security operations) proves the "vertical agent" model works — customers pay for an agent that deeply understands their tool stack and workflows, not another horizontal AI assistant.

Also notable: Qevlar went through Microsoft and Meta accelerator programs. This suggests Big Tech is actively cultivating vertical AI agent plays. Alizé could position similarly — get into a Microsoft/Anthropic ecosystem program for French PME agents.

The Atos and Orange Cyberdefense relationships are interesting — large French system integrators working with vertical AI agents. If Alizé gets traction, similar integrators could be distribution or implementation partners.

## Signal 2: March 2026 French Funding Deep Dive — AI at 74% of All Capital Raised

Maddyness published the full March 2026 funding breakdown. Key numbers: €1.5B across 37 operations. AI companies took €1.115B — 74% of all capital deployed.

**Notable AI rounds (beyond AMI Labs €890M):**
- **Alan** (health insurance): €100M late stage — AI in claims processing/health insurance
- **Qevlar AI**: ~$25.8M (mentioned in Maddyness data)
- **Waiv** (AI payments): €24.3M
- **Parallel** (AI infrastructure): €17.4M
- **Escape** (AI security testing): €15.6M

**Structural insight:** March 2026 had 3 late-stage deals (vs 0 in March 2025). This signals the French VC market is opening up for companies that have proven product-market fit — not just early-stage speculation. The median deal is getting bigger for companies that can show traction.

**What this means for Alizé:**
The funding environment strongly favors AI companies that can show real deployments. Alizé should prioritize showing pilot revenue and customer testimonials over raw technology differentiation. French investors (and customers) want proof the agent works in a real French business.

## Signal 3: Omniscient Decision Intelligence — Seed Round for AI-Powered Reputation Management

Omniscient (pre-seed, Seedcamp) is building an AI-powered decision intelligence platform to protect corporate reputation. Founded by two former McKinsey consultants.

**Key facts:**
- Focus: helping executives anticipate and manage reputation crises
- AI-driven analysis of sentiment, risk signals, stakeholder mapping
- Pre-seed by Seedcamp (top European seed fund)

**What this means for Alizé:**
This is a adjacent space to Alizé's governance angle. Reputation management + AI is a natural extension of "AI that operates correctly and is supervised." If Alizé's agents are making decisions inside business tools, the governance layer could include audit trails, compliance monitoring, and decision explanation — all of which feed into corporate reputation management.

Not a direct competitor, but a potential integration or partnership target for Alizé's governance offering.

## Signal 4: ManoMano Founders Exit — French Ecommerce in Turmoil

ManoMano's founders (Christian Raisson and Philippe de Chanville) are leaving operational roles after years of difficulties — layoffs, growth slowdown. The French DIY/garden ecommerce platform has been struggling against Amazon and Leroy Merlin's own digital transformation.

**What this means for Alizé:**
Not a direct Alizé signal, but a data point about French ecommerce/retail market dynamics. Companies like ManoMano are under pressure to cut costs and improve efficiency — exactly the use case for AI agent automation in back-office operations (customer service, order management, accounting).

The broader implication: French companies in competitive sectors are under margin pressure. AI agent automation that reduces operational costs is increasingly urgent, not nice-to-have.

## Immediate Actions for Alizé

1. **Study Qevlar's go-to-market**: They went through Microsoft and Meta accelerators, landed Atos and Orange Cyberdefense as clients, raised $30M doing vertical AI agents in security. This is the playbook Alizé should replicate in French back-office work.

2. **Add governance narrative to Alizé positioning**: Qevlar's "agentic security" shows that vertical domain expertise + autonomous action + human supervision = enterprise value. Alizé's governance layer (audit trails, compliance, decision explanation) should be front and center — it's what differentiates Alizé from generic AI coworker.

3. **Domain remains the #1 blocker**: alize.ai or alize.fr. Louis needs to decide. This has been blocking the brand for 3+ weeks.

4. **Pilot target: 1 French company with real back-office pain**: The funding environment (74% AI, late-stage opening) means investors and customers want proof. Alizé's first pilot customer is more important than the domain name right now.
