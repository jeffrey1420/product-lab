## Signal 2: Belitsoft AI Agent Development Forecast 2026 — The Coordination Gap

**Source:** Belitsoft / Barchart (April 8, 2026)

Belitsoft published an AI agent development forecast drawing on the Futurum Group's 1H 2026 Enterprise Software Decision Maker Survey. Key findings:

**The numbers:**
- Enterprises are now running an average of **12 AI agents** simultaneously
- Approximately **40% of enterprise applications** will include task-specific agents by end of 2026
- However, roughly **50% of agents still operate in isolation** — no cross-agent communication or coordination
- The shift is from "single AI helper" to "groups of coordinated agents," but the coordination layer is missing

**Why this matters:**

12 agents per enterprise, 50% working alone — that's the orchestration gap. Each agent does its task but doesn't know what the other agents are doing. No shared context, no handoffs, no cross-validation.

This is exactly the problem that multi-agent orchestration platforms solve. And it's the gap between "having AI agents" and "having AI that works."

**What this means for Alizé:**

Alizé's managed service model implicitly assumes orchestration — you're not just deploying one agent, you're deploying a governed workflow that may involve multiple agents, multiple handoffs, and multiple human checkpoints.

The Belitsoft data validates the market need:
- Enterprises want agents (12 average, growing)
- Agents are proliferating but operating in silos
- The failure mode is lack of coordination, not lack of agents

**Product direction signal:** Alizé should explicitly position as a multi-agent orchestration layer with human governance. Not just "deploying AI" but "orchestrating AI across your business with containment rails." The Belitsoft data makes this concrete: most enterprises already have agents; what they don't have is the coordination layer.

## Signal 3: Donecle — AI Agents Moving Into Physical World Operations

**Source:** Maddyness (April 15, 2026)

Donecle (Toulouse-based aerospace inspection startup) raised €10M from IRDI Capital and SWEN Capital Partners to scale its drone+AI aircraft inspection system. Key details:

- 50+ drones deployed across 15 countries
- Lufthansa, United Airlines, LATAM, DHL, French and British military as clients
- AI analyzes drone imagery to detect paint damage, impacts, anomalies
- Reduces aircraft turnaround from 8-16 hours to 2 hours
- Planning predictive maintenance software in 2027

**Why this matters for Alizé:**

Donecle illustrates the next wave of AI agent deployment: **physical-world operations**, not just digital workflows. An AI agent that controls a drone, interacts with the physical world, and operates in regulated industries (aerospace, defense) amplifies the governance problem.

Gartner's 40% AI failure rate becomes a physical safety issue when agents operate drones, inspect aircraft, or manage infrastructure. Alizé's human-in-the-loop and containment model is even more critical in physical-world deployments.

**Product direction signal:** Alizé should consider physical-world AI governance as a future vertical. Donecle's model — autonomous inspection + AI + physical action — is a preview of where the market is going. Governance of physical AI agents (drone fleets, maintenance robots, autonomous vehicles) will be a massive market.

**Alizé angle:** "You trust AI to inspect your aircraft. But who supervises the agent that controls the drone? We do."
