## Signal 1: Vercel Breached by ShinyHunters — Agentic AI Workloads Exposed

**Source:** Decipher (April 19, 2026), HN item #47824976 (214 points, 2h ago)

Vercel disclosed a security breach of internal systems on Sunday. A "limited subset" of customers affected. Incident response team engaged, law enforcement notified. Posts online link the intrusion to **ShinyHunters** — a threat group known for targeting SaaS platforms via SSO/MFA abuse and social engineering, then selling access on criminal forums.

**Critical detail:** Vercel "has a number of offerings focused on agentic AI workloads" — per the article. This is a deployment platform that hosts production AI agents, and it just got popped.

**Alizé angle:**

AI agents deployed on Vercel (or any PaaS) create a new attack surface: the platform itself becomes the pivot point. If ShinyHunters accessed Vercel's internal systems, they potentially have visibility into customer AI workloads, environment variables, API keys, and deployment configs.

For Alizé's prospect conversations: the question is no longer "could your AI agent be compromised" — it's "is your AI deployment platform secure." Every company deploying AI agents on a third-party platform is trusting that platform's internal security. Vercel just showed that trust is fragile.

**Takeaway:** Agentic AI workloads create a compound attack surface — model security + application security + deployment platform security. Alizé's governance layer should include a deployment security audit component.

## Signal 2: Notion Leaks All Editor Email Addresses from Public Pages

**Source:** HN item #47824945 (134 points, 2h ago), via Twitter/weezerOSINT

A vulnerability in Notion exposed the email addresses of every editor on any public page — regardless of the editor's privacy settings. The data was accessible without authentication.

**Alizé angle:**

Notion is a common tool in French SMEs — knowledge bases, wikis, project management. The fact that a widely-trusted SaaS tool silently leaks personal data has two implications:

1. **Data handling compliance**: French companies using Notion for client-facing or internal documentation may have inadvertently exposed personal data. CNIL implications.
2. **Trust signal**: Companies are increasingly aware that "the tools we use" have security surface areas they don't control. Alizé's promise to audit and govern AI toolchains resonates with this anxiety.
