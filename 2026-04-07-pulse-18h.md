## Signal 1: The French PME Buying Committee — Smaller Than Enterprise, But Not Solo

The standard B2B software buying process involves 6-10 decision makers (per JDN data). In French PME/ETI, the committee is structurally smaller but politically complex:

**Typical buying committee for a €500-2,000/month AI managed service:**

| Role | Involvement | Priority |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **DG / PDG** | Final sign-off. Initiates the search when pain becomes acute. | #1 |
| **DAF / Responsable administratif** | Evaluates cost, ROI, budget compatibility. Can block. | #1 |
| **Responsable métier / Operations** | End user of the agent. Champions internally if convinced. | #2 |
| **Informatique / DSI (if exists)** | Security review, integration approval. Often absent in micro-PME. | #3 |
| **Compta / Expert-comptable** | May be consulted for Sage/Pennylane integration decisions. | #3 |

**Key structural insight:** In companies under 50 employees, the DG is often the economic buyer, the user, and the security reviewer simultaneously. This simplifies the sales cycle but raises the stakes — one person kills the deal.

**For Alizé:** The sales deck needs to speak to two distinct personas in the same meeting: the DG (ROI, risk, strategic narrative) and the DAF (cost, implementation, measurable outcomes). Most AI vendors lead with technology. Alizé should lead with cost recovery math.

## Signal 2: The French PME AI Buyer — Profile and Psychology

**Who is actually buying AI tools in French PME right now:**

Based on the Agentova data (60K+ French users, strong TPE/micro-PME adoption) and broader market signals:

**Early AI adopters in French PME are:**
- **TPE owners aged 35-55** who read tech press, attend salons professionnels, and are actively worried about competitive parity
- **DAFs in 50-200 person companies** hunting for cost reduction after 2-3 difficult years (post-Covid, inflation, labor shortages)
- **Operations managers** drowning in repetitive tasks who discovered AI through ChatGPT and are now looking for enterprise-grade versions
- **Young DGsalaries with technical comfort** who discovered AI agents through personal use and want to bring them into the business

**What they have in common:**
- They self-educate online (Google, YouTube, LinkedIn posts from French tech influencers)
- They distrust vendor marketing and trust peer recommendations
- They fear being the first in their industry to try something (innovation uncertainty) AND being the last (competitive obsolescence)
- They have real budget authority at the margin (€500-2,000/month is within DG discretionary budget in many PME) but need justification above €3,000/month

**What they fear most:**
1. **Choosing wrong and being stuck** — vendor lock-in, migration cost, internal switching cost
2. **AI making expensive errors with no accountability** — the invoice agent that pays the wrong supplier
3. **Employee resistance** — the team that refuses to work with AI tools
4. **Hidden costs** — per-token pricing that explodes the budget
5. **Data confidentiality** — French companies are increasingly sensitive to data leaving French/European infrastructure

## Signal 3: The B2B Sales Cycle for AI Managed Services (French PME Context)

**Standard B2B sales cycle phases and timelines:**

| Phase | Description | Typical Duration |
|-------|-------------|------------------|
| **1. Problem Recognition** | DG/operations feels the pain (time wasted, errors, cost bleeding). Stumbles on AI as a potential solution. | 2-8 weeks (variable) |
| **2. Initial Search** | Googles "AI agent PME," asks on LinkedIn, talks to peers. Shortlists 2-4 vendors. | 1-3 weeks |
| **3. Evaluation** | Demos requested. Often 2-3 vendors simultaneously. Technical person tests integration. | 2-6 weeks |
| **4. Economic Validation** | ROI calculation. DAF gets involved. Pricing negotiation. | 1-4 weeks |
| **5. Decision** | DG signs off. Often requires internal justification memo. | 1-3 weeks |
| **6. Pilot / Proof of Concept** | 30-day pilot with defined success metrics. Low commitment, high stakes. | 30-60 days |
| **7. Contract / Rollout** | Flat retainer agreed. Monthly review cadence established. | Week 1-4 |

**Total cycle: 2-4 months from first contact to signed contract** for a managed service at €800-1,500/month.

**What accelerates the cycle:**
- Peer referral (existing customer introduces new prospect)
- Immediate ROI demonstration (actual numbers, not projections)
- Willingness to do a constrained pilot at a fixed price
- Evidence of similar clients (same industry, same size)
- French-language documentation and support

**What kills the deal:**
- Demo that requires significant customization to show value
- Pricing that feels opaque or unpredictable (per-token confusion)
- Technical objection about Sage/Cegid/Pennylane integration that can't be answered
- Missing security credentials (no GDPR compliance documentation, no data residency answer)
- DG who discovers the DAF has been bypassed and gets defensive

## Signal 4: Objection Map — French PME AI Buying Objections

**The 6 objections that come up in almost every AI managed service sale to French PME:**

### Objection 1: "Ça a l'air bien, mais on n'a pas le temps de changer nos processus"
**Root cause:** Change management fear. The team is already overwhelmed.
**Alizé response:** "We handle the implementation. You keep doing your job during the pilot. We measure the ROI before asking for any commitment."

### Objection 2: "On a déjà essayé des outils IA, ça n'a pas donné grand-chose"
**Root cause:** Failed AI pilot from a generic tool (ChatGPT team plan, etc.). Disillusionment.
**Alizé response:** "That's exactly why we work differently — we build the workflow for your specific context, we measure it, and we stay accountable to the results. Not a tool you configure, a service you receive."

### Objection 3: "C'est combien exactement? On a eu des surprises avec les factures d'IA"
**Root cause:** Per-token pricing anxiety. They heard about the OpenAI bill shock stories.
**Alizé response:** "Flat monthly fee. Everything included. We send you one invoice per month, no surprises."

### Objection 4: "Et si l'agent fait une erreur et traite une facture de 40k€ au mauvais fournisseur?"
**Root cause:** Error accountability. No one wants to explain to the DG why the AI paid the wrong vendor.
**Alizé response:** "Every agent action is logged. Every threshold above X€ requires human validation. You have full audit trail and override capability. The AI works, but you never lose control."

### Objection 5: "Nos données comptables sont sensibles. Où vont-elles?"
**Root cause:** Data residency and confidentiality. Especially acute for finance/HR data.
**Alizé response:** "Data processed on French/European infrastructure. We sign a DPA. No training on your data. Full GDPR compliance documentation available."

### Objection 6: "On travaille avec Sage (or Cegid, Pennylane). L'outil va vraiment s'intégrer?"
**Root cause:** Technical integration doubt. They've been burned by "works with Sage!" promises before.
**Alizé response:** "We have MCP connectors for Sage, Cegid, and Pennylane. Here's the demo on [their specific tool]. We've deployed this exact integration for [similar client]."

## Signal 5: Target Customer Personas for Alizé

Based on all accumulated research, three primary personas:

### Persona 1: "La PME de 20-50 salariés, le DG opéré"
**Profile:**
- 20-50 employees, CA €5-30M
- DG is hands-on, often ex-engineer or ex-sales, knows technology but doesn't have a tech team
- Biggest pain: Operations founder syndrome — DG doing €2,000/month of repetitive tasks because the team is too small or too expensive to hire for
- Existing tools: Sage Business Cloud, Pennylane, Excel, some Google Sheets
- No dedicated IT person

**What triggers the search:**
- DG reads about AI agents in the press, realizes they could automate their own repetitive work
- Peer mentions it at a dîner d'entreprise
- Accountant mentions a competitor is using AI for invoicing

**What closes them:**
- Clear personal ROI: "This frees you 8 hours/week to focus on growing the business"
- Simple pricing: flat monthly fee
- Evidence: another DG in their industry using it successfully

**Budget authority:** €600-1,200/month (within DG discretionary spend, no board approval needed)

---

### Persona 2: "L'ETI de 50-200 salariés, la DAF qui cherche des économies"
**Profile:**
- 50-200 employees, CA €30-150M
- DAF (CFO-equivalent) is the champion; DG is the final approver
- Biggest pain: Invoice processing backlog, supplier payment follow-up, month-end close taking too long
- Existing tools: Sage Enterprise, Cegid, SAP Business One, possibly a BI layer (Power BI, Tableur)
- Has IT but IT is overloaded with daily maintenance

**What triggers the search:**
- DAF reads about AI-driven automation in a finance publication or LinkedIn post
- Board is pressuring for cost reduction
- Month-end close metrics are embarrassing (too many manual corrections)

**What closes them:**
- ROI calculation: "Invoice processing costs your team X hours/month. At your blended cost, that's €Y/month. Our service costs €Z/month."
- Control and audit trail: "Here's exactly what the agent did, every day"
- Reference client in same industry

**Budget authority:** €1,500-3,000/month (requires DAF + DG sign-off, usually a procurement process)

---

### Persona 3: "La TPE de 5-15 salariés, l'artisan-digital qui veut grossir"
**Profile:**
- 5-15 employees, CA €0.5-5M
- DG is often the founder, highly digital, discovered AI through personal use
- Biggest pain: Too many tasks, not enough people, everything falls on the DG
- Existing tools: Pennylane or Sage, Gmail/Google Workspace, no real ERP

**What triggers the search:**
- DG used ChatGPT for personal tasks and got excited
- Sees a competitor doing something impressive and wants to catch up
- Reads about Agentova or similar and wants "the business version"

**What closes them:**
- Speed: "We can be live in 2 weeks"
- Peer proof: "Here's what another business like yours saved in 60 days"
- Low commitment: "Start with a 30-day pilot, see the numbers, then decide"

**Budget authority:** €300-800/month (DG discretionary, no approval needed)

## Signal 6: The Alizé Sales Motion — What Works

Based on accumulated market intelligence:

**What works for selling AI managed services to French PME:**

1. **Direct outbound by the founder(s)** — Louis and Gabin should be doing the first calls themselves. PME buyers want to talk to the people who actually build/run the service, not a sales rep. This is Alizé's advantage over larger vendors.

2. **The ROI-first demo** — Start every demo with the prospect's numbers, not the product's features. "You told me you have 200 invoices/month. Your team spends 45 minutes each. At €35/hour, that's €2,625/month in staff time. Here's what our agent would do with those 200 invoices."

3. **Constrained 30-day pilot** — Fix the scope, fix the price, define the success metric in writing. At the end of 30 days, show the actual numbers. Close or walk — no pressure.

4. **Industry-specific social proof** — "Here's what we did for a similaires company in [industry]." One successful reference in their industry is worth more than 10 generic case studies.

5. **The governance narrative** — Every demo should include the audit trail demo. Show them exactly what they can see, what gets logged, what requires human approval. This is the "enterprise grade" story that separates Alizé from Agentova.

6. **French-language everything** — Documentation, reporting, support, invoices — all in French. This is not optional for this market. It signals seriousness and respect.

## Strategic Implications for Alizé

1. **The pilot is the product** — For most PME buyers, the first 30 days IS the sale. The pilot must be scoped for guaranteed measurable success, not impressive complexity. Simple, well-measured, clearly ROI-positive.

2. **DG + DAF are two different sales motions** — The DG wants strategic narrative (competitive parity, freed time, modern business). The DAF wants a spreadsheet (cost of current state, cost of service, ROI payback period). Have both ready in every meeting.

3. **Flat pricing eliminates the #1 objection** — Per-token anxiety is the most common blocker for PME buyers who have heard horror stories. A flat monthly fee removes the variable risk entirely. Alizé should publish clear pricing from day one.

4. **The audit trail demo closes skeptics** — French PME buyers who are on the fence about AI often come around when they see the operational transparency. The governance story isn't just compliance theater — it's a sales tool.

5. **Industry verticalization accelerates sales** — A reference client in the prospect's industry cuts the evaluation phase in half. Alizé should prioritize pilots in concentrated industry clusters (e.g., 5 accounting firms, 3 logistics companies) rather than spreading across too many verticals.
