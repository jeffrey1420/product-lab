## Alizé Buyer Personas

### Persona 1: "Marie-Pierre" — The Overwhelmed Gérante de PME

**Profile:**
- **Role:** Gérante / Dirigeante (often owner-founder)
- **Company:** TPE/PME, 10–50 employees
- **Sector:** Service businesses (consulting, accounting, legal, property management, healthcare administration)
- **Revenue:** €500K–€10M
- **Age:** 40–60
- **Location:** Provincial France (Caen, Rennes, Nantes, Dijon, etc.) — not Paris
- **Tech maturity:** Medium-low. Uses tools daily but not deeply technical. Uses Ciel, Pennylane, or equivalent accounting software. Google Workspace or Microsoft 365. Probably has a CRM they don't fully use (or no CRM at all).
- **Language of preference:** French

**Pain Points:**
1. **Operational chaos from dispersed tools:** Marie-Pierre juggles 5–10 different software tools (email, calendar, invoicing, project management, client communication). Data lives in silos. She manually copies information from one tool to another multiple times per day.
2. **Repetitive admin tasks consuming her best hours:** Contract review, client onboarding, quote follow-ups, reporting — all manual. She works 50+ hour weeks but feels like she's doing clerical work, not growing the business.
3. **No internal IT or AI expertise:** Cannot evaluate AI tools, doesn't have a CTO, cannot hire one (budget/talent shortage). Any technical project requires external consultants she doesn't trust.
4. **EU AI Act anxiety without understanding it:** Has heard about the regulation, knows it applies to her eventually, but has no idea what it means operationally. Afraid of fines but paralyzed by complexity.
5. **Vendor overload:** Has been approached by 10+ AI vendors. She's skeptical of buzzwords, doesn't understand what they're selling, and distrusts anyone who overpromises.

**Decision-Making Process:**
- Not an impulsive buyer. Needs references from people she trusts (other business owners in her network, her accountant, her industry association).
- Decision is made emotionally ("this person gets my business") and justified rationally ("the numbers make sense").
- Sales cycle: 2–6 weeks, short because she doesn't have committee-based decision-making — she's the owner, she decides.
- Budget authority: full. Doesn't need board approval for €500–€2000/month decisions.

**Preferred Channels:**
- LinkedIn (passive — follows industry groups, sees peer activity)
- Industry associations (CPF, FFP, regional business networks)
- Word of mouth from other entrepreneurs
- Google ("PME IA outil gestion")
- Email from trusted referral (not cold email from unknown vendor)

**Quote that reveals her mindset:**
*"J'ai déjà essayé des outils IA, ça me promet monts et merveilles et en vrai ça ne fait rien ou alors il faut que je forme toute mon équipe pour trois choses. Moi ce qu'il me faut, c'est que quelqu'un me dise : on s'en occupe, ça marche, vous supervisez."*

**What closes the deal for Marie-Pierre:**
- A concrete, visible pilot with her actual data, showing measurable time savings in the first 2 weeks
- A French-speaking contact person she can call directly
- A clear compliance guarantee (EU AI Act, French data hosting)
- Flat monthly pricing with no surprise invoices

**What kills the deal:**
- Technical jargon in the pitch
- Mentioning "prompts" or "agents" — she doesn't know what that means
- Demo with fake/sample data instead of her actual workflow
- Multi-month commitment before seeing results

### Persona 2: "Thierry" — The IT-Decision-Maker-in-Spite-of-Himself (PME with IT)

**Profile:**
- **Role:** Gérant with IT responsibility, or Dirigeant + DSI informal (sometimes a "référent numérique" or a key employee trusted with tech decisions)
- **Company:** PME/ETI, 50–250 employees
- **Sector:** Distribution, manufacturing, B2B services, logistics
- **Revenue:** €10M–€50M
- **Age:** 35–55
- **Location:** Anywhere in France

**Pain Points:**
1. **AI is a board/headquarters directive but he has no idea how to execute it:** Parent company or board has said "we need an AI strategy." Thierry is the one tasked with making it happen but has no AI background.
2. **Existing vendor landscape is messy:** Multiple disconnected systems (ERP, CRM, WMS, legacy tools). Integration is painful and expensive with traditional IT approaches.
3. **AI pilots that died:** Already ran 1–3 AI experiments that either didn't scale or produced underwhelming results. Skeptical but not closed-minded.
4. **EU AI Act compliance audit risk:** His company's AI systems could be flagged in an audit. Needs to demonstrate governance and audit trails, especially for automated decision-making in HR, credit, or customer-facing processes.
5. **Budget constraints vs. headquarters expectations:** Must deliver AI initiatives but capital budget is constrained. Prefers operational expenditure (OPEX) over capital expenditure (CAPEX).

**Decision-Making Process:**
- More formal than Marie-Pierre. Will likely involve his accountant or external consultant for ROI validation.
- Needs a clear vendor comparison (Alizé vs. doing it in-house vs. a big consultancy).
- Requires SLA, data processing agreement (DPA), and EU AI Act compliance documentation.
- Decision cycle: 1–3 months.

**Preferred Channels:**
- Google search for relevant terms ("IAagent PME compliance EU AI Act")
- LinkedIn (both passive content and direct outreach)
- Industry events / webinaires
- Peer referrals from other DSI or dirigeants in his network
- Inbound from Alizé content (blog posts about EU AI Act compliance for PME)

**Quote that reveals his mindset:**
*"On m'a mis là-dessus et on m'a dit 'fais-nous une roadmap IA'. Le problème c'est que je n'ai pas le budget d'un cabinet de conseil et que les outils que je trouve me parlent soit de prompt engineering soit d'agents autonomes — en termes concrets pour mon ERP SAGE je ne sais pas ce que ça veut dire."*

**What closes the deal for Thierry:**
- Integration with his existing stack (SAGE, Cegid, Salesforce, etc.) via MCP or API
- A documented EU AI Act compliance framework that maps to his existing systems
- Proof of similar deployments in his sector
- Month-to-month or 6-month contract with clear KPIs
- A named technical contact who understands both AI and French business culture

**What kills the deal:**
- Vendor that can't explain concretely how the AI agent accesses his existing data
- Promises of "full automation" — he knows better
- Lock-in with opaque proprietary integrations
- No clear escalation path when things break

### Persona 3: "Philippe" — The ambitious DG of an ETI seeking operational leverage

**Profile:**
- **Role:** Directeur Général / PDG of an ETI, 250–1000 employees
- **Sector:** Financial services, insurance, energy, logistics, healthcare
- **Revenue:** €50M–€500M
- **Age:** 45–60
- **Location:** Paris or major regional city (Lyon, Marseille, Toulouse)

**Pain Points:**
1. **Scale pressure with a flat or shrinking headcount:** Philippe needs to grow revenue without proportionally growing costs. Every department is under pressure to do more with less.
2. **Repetitive processes creating bottlenecks:** Claims processing, contract intake, compliance reporting, client onboarding — high-volume, rules-based workflows that are prime candidates for AI agents.
3. **EU AI Act is a board-level concern:** Philippe personally heard about the regulation from his legal counsel. He's worried about the reputational and financial risk of non-compliance, especially if his company uses automated decision-making in high-risk areas.
4. **Has been approached by Big Tech and doesn't trust them:** Microsoft, Google, and Salesforce have all pitched him AI solutions. He's wary of data sovereignty issues and doesn't want to be locked into a US hyperscaler's ecosystem.
5. **Needs a partner, not a vendor:** Philippe doesn't have time to project-manage an AI deployment. He wants someone who will assess, deploy, operate, and report — a managed service.

**Decision-Making Process:**
- Most formal buyer of the three. Will involve DSI, Direction Juridique, Direction Financière.
- Requires RFP or at least competitive evaluation (2–3 vendors).
- Needs a committee to approve contract above ~€10K/month.
- Decision cycle: 3–6 months.

**Preferred Channels:**
- Direct outreach via executive network (mutual connections, board members, industry events)
- Consultancy referrals (especially from firms Philippe already trusts for EU AI Act advice)
- Incoming through France Digitale or similar ETI-focused networks
- Premium content: white papers on EU AI Act compliance, ETI case studies with quantified ROI
- Alizé thought leadership that positions the company as the "French managed AI service for regulated industries"

**Quote that reveals his mindset:**
*"Ce qui m'intéresse c'est un partenaire qui comprend la contrainte réglementaire française, qui peut déployer rapidement sur nos processus claims, et qui m'accompagne dans la durée. Je ne veux pas d'un outil, je veux un service."*

**What closes the deal for Philippe:**
- EU AI Act compliance by design (audit trail, human validation gates, CNIL-ready)
- French/EU data hosting (OVHcloud or equivalent) — explicitly important
- Reference customers in his sector with quantified results (e.g., "reduced claims processing time by 40%")
- A pilot phase that produces measurable ROI before full deployment
- A governance framework with monthly reporting to his board

**What kills the deal:**
- US hyperscaler reference (even implicitly — he doesn't want Microsoft Azure AI in his compliance report)
- Vague ROI projections without methodology
- A vendor that undersells technical complexity and overdelivers on promises
- Inability to provide a legal data processing agreement under French/EU law

## Cross-Persona Synthesis: What Alizé Must Nail

**Common to all three personas:**
- French-speaking, French-based operations
- EU AI Act compliance is now a concern (Marie-Pierre is anxious about it; Thierry needs documentation; Philippe requires it)
- All three distrust buzzwords and need concrete, sector-relevant proof points
- None have internal AI/agent expertise — they want someone to own the operational complexity
- Preferred sales motion: trust-based referral > cold outreach; pilot before contract; visible results within 2 weeks

**Segment priorities:**
- Marie-Pierre (TPE/PME, 10–50 employees): largest volume of potential customers, fastest sales cycle, needs the most hand-holding, but also most likely to refer peers
- Thierry (PME, 50–250 employees): medium volume, medium cycle, needs integration clarity and compliance docs, can unlock industry-specific use cases
- Philippe (ETI, 250–1000 employees): lowest volume but highest contract value, longest cycle, but establishes Alizé's credibility as an enterprise-grade operator

**Priority go-to-market segments:**
1. **Priority 1 (fastest revenue):** Marie-Pierre in service sectors (expertise-comptable, property management, insurance intermediaries) — high pain, simple workflows, referral-friendly
2. **Priority 2 (scalable):** Thierry in manufacturing/distribution — larger teams, multiple workflow candidates, higher contract value
3. **Priority 3 (credibility):** Philippe in regulated ETI (financial services, insurance) — slowest to close but establishes Alizé as a serious enterprise player
