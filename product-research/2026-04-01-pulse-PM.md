## Signal 1: Mistral AI's Paris Data Center — Q2 2026, 13,800 Nvidia GB300 GPUs

**The message from CEO Arthur Mensch:** "Scaling our infrastructure in Europe is critical to empower our customers and to ensure AI innovation and autonomy remain at the heart of Europe."

**For Alizé:** This is the strongest validation yet. Mistral is building sovereign European AI infrastructure with French government backing. When Alizé pitches "EU data residency with Mistral," it's not a startup gamble — it's a funded, bank-backed infrastructure story. ## Signal 3: 1-Bit LLMs Are Now Commercially Viable

PrismML released **1-Bit Bonsai** — three model sizes (1.7B, 4B, 8B) with 1-bit weights. The 8B model fits in 1.15 GB of memory, runs 8x faster, and is 5x more energy efficient than full-precision 8B models, while matching benchmark performance. The 1.7B model runs at 130 tokens/second on an iPhone 17 Pro Max.

This is a step change in deployment economics. 1-bit models are not a research curiosity — they're a commercial product targeting robotics, real-time agents, and edge computing.

**For Alizé:** Not immediately relevant but worth tracking. If 1-bit models reach 30B+ parameter sizes at similar efficiency gains, they could disrupt the inference cost equation for enterprise agents. A 30B model in 4GB instead of 60GB changes the device-edge deployment story. Alizé should benchmark 1-bit Mistral equivalents when available. The inference cost advantage of small, efficient models aligns with Alizé's "cost-conscious French PME" positioning.

## Signal 4: OpenAI's $852B Valuation Masks Real Pressure

OpenAI closed a $122B funding round at a $852B post-money valuation (March 31). $2B/month revenue. 900M weekly active users. But: Sora shut down, features being cut, not profitable. The ad business hit $100M ARR in 2 months — a new revenue stream that raises questions about where OpenAI's focus lies.

Key tension: the $852B valuation requires sustained capital-intensive buildout while OpenAI simultaneously cuts costs and explores ads. The IPO pressure is real.

**For Alizé:** OpenAI's struggles with profitability validate Alizé's managed service approach. "We deploy agents that run 24/7 with clear ROI" is the counter-positioning. Also: OpenAI's focus on ads and consumer products creates more whitespace for vertical AI agents in the enterprise.
