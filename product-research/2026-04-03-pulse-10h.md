## Signal 1: Deloitte — SaaS Enters the "Agentification" Era (2026-2030)

Deloitte's 2026 SaaS + AI Agents prediction report (November 2025, still highly relevant) lays out the structural shift that's now beginning:

**Key numbers:**
- 57% of organizations put 21-50% of digital transformation budgets into AI automation
- 20% put 50%+ ($700M average for $13B revenue companies)
- 75% of surveyed leaders funded agentic AI in the last 12 months
- Deloitte predicts up to 50% of organizations will put 50%+ of digital transformation budgets toward AI automation in 2026

**Pricing revolution incoming:**
- Subscriptions and seat-based licensing → hybrid usage- and outcome-based pricing
- This is a fundamental threat to traditional SaaS revenue models
- For Alizé: this validates outcome-based pricing as the right long-term model

**The 5-year horizon:**
- Deloitte: "entire enterprise applications could be replaced by agents" — but not in 2026
- It will take 5+ years for full "agentification" of complex workflows
- This gives Alizé a real window: the transition period (now through 2030) is where the managed service + governance layer creates maximum value

**SaaS vendors are taking two paths:**
1. Build native agent capabilities into existing products (Salesforce AgentForce, ServiceNow, etc.)
2. Let agents orchestrate across multiple SaaS tools (the Alizé positioning)

**What this means for Alizé:**
The pricing model shift is the most important insight. Alizé should not price like a SaaS tool (per seat, per month). It should price like a managed service: per outcome, per workflow automated, or a fixed monthly retainer with clear ROI. This aligns with the "pilot first" approach — if the first pilot can demonstrate cost savings or revenue impact, outcome pricing becomes possible.

## Signal 2: US Government Bans Anthropic (Claude) — Geopolitical AI Splintering Accelerates

In February 2026, the US government ordered federal agencies to stop using Anthropic/Claude products. This is being called the "first US ban of a major AI company." The geopolitical implications are significant:

- **France/EU context**: France has been actively building "sovereign AI" infrastructure (Mistral AI at €830M, AMI Labs at €890M, Paris data center). The US ban on Claude reinforces the French government and enterprise preference for European AI providers.
- **What this means for Alizé**: If French enterprises and PME are actively steered away from US AI providers, Mistral AI and other European providers become the default. Alizé's agent deployment layer, if built on Mistral or European models, becomes a "sovereign AI" story — a strong positioning hook for government-adjacent or regulated industries in France.
- **Cowork product impact**: Anthropic's Cowork product (already covered) is effectively blocked from US federal use. European alternatives like Alizé benefit from this displacement.

## Signal 3: Shift Technology — Agent-Based AI for Insurance Validates Vertical Architecture

Shift Technology (French, $540M raised) uses agent-based AI specifically for insurance workflows:

**What they do:**
- Agent-based AI for claims processing: faster claim processing, accurate liability assessment
- Fraud detection, case investigation management
- Risk underwriting with agent-based models
- Healthcare payment accuracy

**Why this matters for Alizé:**
Shift is the French proof-of-concept for "vertical agents + specific workflow outcomes." Their $540M raise and operational scale (they serve insurers globally) validates that the agent architecture (multiple specialized agents working together) is production-proven in regulated industries.

The insurance sector and the "PME with repetitive tasks" sector have the same fundamental need: agents that can operate reliably within specific domain rules, with audit trails and accountability.

**What Alizé can learn from Shift:**
- Shift charges by outcome (claims processed, fraud detected) — this is the pricing model Alizé should emulate
- Shift's agents are specialized by function (underwriting agent, fraud agent, claims agent) — Alizé's architecture for each client should follow the same specialization pattern
- Shift operates in a regulated industry — French PME are increasingly regulated on data (RGPD), AI usage, etc. Alizé's governance layer is essentially the "compliance scaffolding" for agents operating in these conditions

**Key details:**

- Crowdfunding on Sowefund alongside the €1.7M already closed

**What this means for Alizé:**
Not a direct competitor, but a useful reference point for how the French healthcare system is opening to digital therapeutics:
- HAS reimbursement pathway is now proven (Ludocare precedent)
- French patients/consumers are willing to pay for digital health tools
- The "prevention + chronic disease management" model in healthcare maps to "prevention of operational inefficiency + management of repetitive tasks" in business

- Alizé's "governance layer gets projects to production" and "outcome-based pricing" positioning is perfectly aligned with this shift
- The contrast between ManoMano's VC-funded bloat and Alizé's managed-service model could be a useful narrative in pitch materials

## Signal 6: Generare — €20M Series A for Data-Driven Drug Discovery (Biotech)

Generare (French biotech) raised €20M Series A led by Alven and Daphni. Their approach: generating novel molecular data from microbial genomes to accelerate drug discovery.

**Key insight:**
Only 3% of molecular diversity is known. The microbial genome is 97% unexplored. Generare is building the data infrastructure to access this untapped chemical space.

**What this means for Alizé:**
Generare's pitch is "data infrastructure for a field that has been using the same recycled data." This same logic applies to AI agents in French PME — most companies are using the same generic AI tools (ChatGPT, Copilot) without domain-specific training data. Alizé's managed service model should include building proprietary data/workflows per client — that's their competitive moat, just like Generare's molecular data library is theirs.

## Immediate Actions for Alizé

1. **Model provider decision — Mistral AI first**: With Anthropic banned from US federal and under scrutiny in Europe, Mistral AI is the natural choice for a French AI agent company. Alizé should pilot with Mistral's AI Studio + agent framework. This also strengthens the "French sovereign AI" narrative for government-adjacent clients.

2. **Shift Technology as the pricing template**: Shift's per-outcome pricing (claims processed, fraud detected) is exactly the model Alizé should adopt. Don't sell "agent seats" — sell "workflows automated" or "hours saved per month." This is the conversation Louis should have with the first pilot customer.

3. **Domain name — still the #1 blocker**: alize.ai or alize.fr has been open for weeks. Every day without a domain is a day without a brand. This needs to be decided now.

4. **Per-client data moat**: Generare's lesson — proprietary data is the moat. Alizé should build client-specific agent configurations and workflows that are not portable. The more a client depends on Alizé's specific implementation, the stickier the relationship.

5. **First pilot momentum**: The market validation (Deloitte, Shift, Mistral, Qevlar) is overwhelming. Alizé's priority should be getting one French PME to say yes to a pilot. Everything else — domain, pricing, positioning — gets validated by real execution.
