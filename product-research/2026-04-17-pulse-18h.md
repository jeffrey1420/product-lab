## Signal 1: Ledger Builds AI Agent Security Infrastructure — French Sovereign Player Enters the Agent Governance Layer

**Source:** Maddyness, April 15, 2026

Ledger (French crypto hardware wallet unicorn, ~$1.3B valuation) announced a full AI agent security roadmap. This is a significant development because Ledger is a well-funded, serious French tech company making a deliberate bet on AI agent infrastructure.

**The roadmap (2026):**

- **Q2 2026 — Device Management Kit (DMK):** Launched. A developer toolkit for adding human validation checkpoints into AI agent applications. Ledger is already shipping developer tooling.
- **Q2 2026 — AI Agent Identity:** Agents get a hardware-linked identity registered on Ledger's blockchain. This gives each AI agent a tamper-proof identity tied to a physical device.
- **Q3 2026 — Human-in-the-loop approval system:** Agents get spending limits, usage restrictions, and specific permissions. Actions outside those bounds auto-escalate to the human for approval.
- **Q4 2026 — Attestation mechanisms:** Verify the human identity behind every AI agent interaction. Prevents bot proliferation and account multiplication.

**Key hire:** Ian Rogers (former LVMH digital transformation lead, Apple Music development) appointed Chief Human Agency Officer. This is a meaningful title — "Human Agency" explicitly frames the design problem as preserving human control over autonomous systems.

**What this means for Alizé:**

1. **Ledger just validated the human-in-the-loop + governance layer thesis.** A serious French company is building exactly the pattern Alizé proposes: hardware-secured identity, human approval gates, spending limits, attestation. This is convergent validation from an unexpected direction.

2. **Ledger is a potential infrastructure partner, not a competitor.** Ledger's DMK and agent identity layer could be a building block Alizé integrates rather than builds. A French PME using Alizé could leverage Ledger's hardware security for agent authentication.

3. **The "Chief Human Agency Officer" title is worth stealing.** In Alizé's positioning, the governance layer isn't about limiting AI — it's about preserving human agency. Ledger's framing makes this concept legible to enterprise buyers.

4. **Ledger is pursuing a B2B2C model for agent security.** They're selling to developers (DMK) and to enterprises (human-in-the-loop governance). Alizé could be a downstream integrator of this infrastructure for French back-office workflows.

## Signal 3: US AI Labs Move Against Model Distillation — Global AI Sovereignty Tension Rises

**Source:** Chinese discussion threads (知乎/Zhihu), April 8, 2026

OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google jointly announced measures against model distillation — the practice of using a large model's outputs to train a smaller, cheaper model. The三家美国AI巨头 (three US AI giants) are cracking down on distillation as a way to protect their market position.

**What this means:**

1. **Distillation-based open-source models may face legal/technical headwinds.** If US labs successfully restrict distillation, open-source models trained via distillation (a common technique) become riskier to use commercially.

2. **This strengthens the case for training on sovereign infrastructure.** Alizé's plan to use Mistral (European, French-founded) on OVHcloud (European infrastructure) becomes more compelling if US labs are actively working to restrict model replication.

3. **European AI independence accelerates as a strategic imperative.** The US labs' distillation crackdown is another data point showing that French/European companies cannot rely on US AI infrastructure without existential risk. Alizé's sovereign AI positioning is correct.
