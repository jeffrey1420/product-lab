## Signal 1: Microsoft Launches MCP Center — Enterprise MCP Registry Goes Live

Microsoft officially launched **MCP Center** at `mcp.microsoft.com`, a dedicated portal for building, exposing, and managing MCP servers within popular AI development environments. The page explicitly positions MCP as the enterprise integration standard: "Built to seamlessly integrate across MCP hosts. Effortlessly expose and manage these servers within popular MCP hosts."

The MCP Center includes an **Exa MCP** server for web search and content extraction — directly competitive with standalone search tools. Microsoft's backing of the MCP protocol is significant: it signals that MCP is no longer a niche open-source convention but a vendor-backed enterprise standard.

Additional MCP ecosystem growth confirmed by recent launches:
- Medialister's editorial marketplace MCP (covered in last pulse)
- MCP servers for Notion, Slack, GitHub, Salesforce all seeing active development

**What this means for Alizé:**
MCP-first is the right bet. Microsoft's formalization of MCP as an enterprise integration layer validates the core technical strategy — connect Claude to PME tools via MCP, wrap it in governance, deliver as managed service. The question is no longer "is MCP the right approach" but "who wins the MCP managed services market." Alizé's positioning as the French MCP integrator is defensible.

## Signal 2: 100% of Enterprises Planning to Expand Agentic AI — The CrewAI Survey

CrewAI published its **2026 State of Agentic AI Survey Report**, based on 500 C-level executives at organizations with $100M+ revenue. The headline: **100% of surveyed enterprises plan to expand their use of agentic AI this year.** That is not a trend line — it is a verdict.

Supporting data from KPMG's AI Quarterly Pulse:
- Agent deployment surged from **11% to 42%** of organizations between Q1 and Q3 2025 — nearly 4x in under six months
- Organizations have already automated **31% of workflows** with AI agents, and plan to expand by another **33%** in 2026
- **75%** of respondents report high or very high impact on time savings; **69%** cite significant operational cost reductions

Gartner's 2025 platform forecast adds the architectural context: from under **5% of applications** embedding agent capabilities in 2025 to **40% in 2026** — "the steepest adoption curve in enterprise history."

The market size trajectory: $7.63B in 2025 to $182.97B by 2033.

**What this means for Alizé:**
The market is moving from "should we deploy agents?" to "how do we deploy agents at scale?" That's exactly where managed service providers win. French PME don't have the internal engineering capacity to build agent infrastructure — they need someone to do it for them. Alizé's managed service model is perfectly timed for this inflection.

## Signal 3: The Governance Tax — 40% of Agentic AI Projects at Risk of Cancellation

The same bullish data comes with a significant counter-signal: **over 40% of agentic AI projects are at risk of cancellation by 2027** if governance, observability, and ROI clarity aren't established. System complexity — specifically multi-agent orchestration, reliability, and traceability — has become the primary deployment bottleneck, surpassing model capability as the limiting factor.

Key findings:
- **80% of companies** have already experienced AI applications acting outside intended boundaries
- **93% of IT leaders** plan to introduce autonomous agents within two years; nearly half have already implemented them
- The gap between pilot and production is governance, not technology

Industries moving fastest: Retail (76% increasing investment), Healthcare (68% using agents; up to $150B annual savings potential), Finance ($80B+ projected AI spend in 2025), Manufacturing (77% using AI, up from 70% in 2024).

**What this means for Alizé:**
The governance problem is Alizé's core value proposition. French PME in regulated sectors (healthcare, finance, legal) are particularly exposed to the governance gap. A managed service that includes MCP connectors, usage logging, human oversight loops, and monthly reporting is not overhead — it's the product. This data justifies the governance premium in pricing conversations.

## Signal 4: U.S. Government Bans Anthropic — A Geopolitical Wild Card

In a move described as the first-ever U.S. government sanction against a frontier AI lab, the Trump administration ordered federal agencies to **stop using Anthropic products** (reported February 28, 2026). This follows earlier restrictions on Claude Code usage by Chinese-controlled companies (September 2025).

The framing: Anthropic was labeled a "supply-chain risk" by the U.S. Defense Department, citing the company's broad enterprise customer base and compute infrastructure commitments as potential national security exposure.

Separately, OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google — despite their competition — are sharing intelligence through the Frontier Model Forum to counter **adversarial model distillation** by Chinese AI labs (DeepSeek, Moongshot, MiniMax), whom they collectively accuse of "free-riding on capabilities developed by U.S. frontier labs."

**What this means for Alizé:**
Complex signal. On one hand, Anthropic's exclusion from U.S. government contracts could create pressure on their commercial strategy. On the other, the Defense Department framing suggests the issue is Anthropic's global reach, not its technology quality. For French PME, this reinforces the data sovereignty argument: an AI service that can say "deployed in France, governed by French law, powered by Claude" is insulated from this particular geopolitical risk in a way that a U.S. government-hosted service is not.

## Signal 5: OpenAI's Agentic RPA Roadmap — Computer Use Goes Enterprise

OpenAI's 2026 agent roadmap (per Chinese tech analysis citing internal strategy) focuses on three vectors:

1. **Agentic RPA** — Computer Use applied to enterprise workflow automation, targeting the same back-office automation market as Alizé
2. **Deep Research** — already launched, signals serious investment in research-grade autonomous agents
3. **Operator** — agentic web interaction, broader consumer/enterprise reach

The competitive picture: OpenAI and Anthropic are now directly competing on the agentic enterprise stack. OpenAI has distribution (ChatGPT Enterprise, Microsoft partnership); Anthropic has the safety-first positioning preferred in regulated industries.

**What this means for Alizé:**
OpenAI entering the managed agent space with Microsoft backing is the most credible competitive threat to Alizé's positioning. However, the Microsoft-OpenAI relationship creates a dependency that could limit OpenAI's independence in European markets where Microsoft has its own regulatory exposure. Alizé's independent positioning — not tied to any single model provider or hyperscaler — could be a genuine differentiator for French companies wary of further U.S. concentration in AI infrastructure.
