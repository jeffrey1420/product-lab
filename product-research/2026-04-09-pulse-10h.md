## Signal 1: Mistral AI Publishes Full "European AI Playbook" — 22 Proposals in Detail

Following up on the April 7 policy document flagged in the 06h pulse, L'Usine Digitale published the full details on April 8. "European AI: a playbook to own it" is a comprehensive industrial strategy document organized around four pillars:

**Pillar 1 — Talents:** Ultra-fast European visa for AI researchers/engineers, co-funded doctoral programs, university-industry chairs, and intra-EU talent circulation. Goal: make Europe a global AI training and research hub.

**Pillar 2 — Capital & Corporate Simplification:** Simplify the regulatory jungle (company law fragmentation across EU member states), harmonize stock-option regimes, align EU saving policies toward deeptech/AI funds, and create EU-level "deeptech labels" to channel institutional capital.

**Pillar 3 — Public Procurement as Growth Engine:** Transform government purchasing into a strategic lever. Preferences for European AI in public contracts, simplified SME/startup access to public tenders, tax credits and "vouchers" for AI adoption in PME. This is directly relevant to Alizé's government-adjacent and PME targets.

**Pillar 4 — Infrastructure Sovereignty:** €830M debt raised (March 30) for a Paris data center at Bruyères-le-Châtel with 13,800 GB300 GPUs and 44 MW capacity. Sweden data center announced at €1.2B. Target: 200 MW across Europe by end of 2027. Mistral also proposes mandatory European infrastructure for "critical AI models."

**New: Mistral Forge launch** (mid-March): A service for large enterprises to build custom frontier models from scratch based on proprietary data. This positions Mistral not just as an API provider but as an enterprise AI infrastructure builder — similar to how Alizé wants to position itself as a managed service builder for PME.

**What this means for Alizé:**
If European procurement preferences become real policy, Alizé (French team, French contracts, CNIL-aligned) becomes preferentially eligible over US-based AI service providers. The Forge launch validates Alizé's model: going beyond generic API access to building specialized, fine-tuned solutions on top of European infrastructure. Alizé's positioning as a French managed AI service layer for PME maps directly onto Pillar 3 (PME adoption via vouchers/credits). This is a strong policy tailwind.

## Signal 3: Outcome-Based Pricing for AI Agents — Still Experimental, But Coming

Deloitte's SaaS + AI agents analysis (2026 predictions) highlights that pricing models for AI agents are in flux. The current dominant model — subscription per-seat — is being challenged by:

- **Outcome-based pricing**: pay for actual business results (tickets resolved, employees hired via HR agent, revenue AI contributed to). Currently "still a long way from widespread use."
- **Challenge**: Attribution is hard, especially in multi-agent systems from different vendors. Who gets credit when an agent from Vendor A triggers a workflow in Vendor B's agent?
- **The instrumentation problem**: Billing, observability, and financial compliance will need to become real-time and autonomous. This requires advanced system metering.

**What this means for Alizé:**
Alizé should track this closely. If outcome-based pricing becomes standard, Alizé's managed service model is well-positioned to negotiate value-based contracts (retainer + outcome bonus). However, this requires Alizé to build strong measurability into its deployments from day one — dashboards showing concrete KPI impact per agent. The startups that can demonstrate and attribute AI value will win in 2-3 years. Alizé should invest early in client-facing ROI dashboards and clear success metrics.

## Signal 4: EU AI Act Moves to Implementation — Real Enforcement Timeline

The April 2026 AI digest confirms the EU has moved from the AI Act framework-setting phase to concrete implementation. Key developments:

- **Regulatory compliance is now a first-class AI product requirement**, not an afterthought. Enterprises are increasingly requiring AI vendors to demonstrate compliance architecture before signing contracts.

**What this means for Alizé:**
CNIL compliance, AI Act conformity, and audit trails are not just nice-to-have — they are sales requirements for French enterprise deals. Alizé's "governance and supervision" positioning (agents that work under human oversight, with clear accountability) maps directly to what EU regulators and French DPOs will demand. Alizé should build a compliance checklist/documentation package as a standard deliverable in its deployment offering. This differentiates it from US AI vendors who treat compliance as a checkbox.

Multiple sources confirm the trend toward specialized, vertical AI models:

- **Deloitte**: "companies investing in specialized models tailored for specific industries rather than general-purpose AI, signaling a maturing marketplace."

**What this means for Alizé:**
Alizé's diagnostic → narrow agent deployment → operate model is directionally correct. The market is validating that vertical depth beats horizontal breadth for enterprise AI. Alizé should double down on building pre-configured agent templates for specific French SME workflows (HR onboard, invoice follow-up, regulatory monitoring) rather than building general-purpose agents.
