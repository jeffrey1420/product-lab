## Signal 1: Factory — $150M at $1.5B Valuation for Enterprise AI Coding Agents

**Source:** TechCrunch, April 16, 2026

Factory, a startup developing AI agents for enterprise engineering teams, raised $150M at a $1.5B valuation. The round was led by **Khosla Ventures**, with participation from **Sequoia Capital**, **Insight Partners**, and **Blackstone**. Keith Rabois (Khosla) joined the board.

**Key facts:**
- Founded 2023 by Matan Grinberg (dropped out of PhD at UC Berkeley after cold-emailing Shaun Maguire at Sequoia)
- Key differentiator: **multi-model switching** — Factory switches between Claude (Anthropic), DeepSeek, and other foundation models depending on the task
- Customers: Morgan Stanley, Ernst & Young, Palo Alto Networks
- Total raised to date: approximately $150M in this round (implied seed + Series A before)
- Direct competitors already in market: Anthropic (Claude Code), Cursor, Cognition (Devin)

**Why this matters for Alizé:**

1. **Enterprise AI agents are a validated, well-funded category.** Factory joining Anthropic, Cursor, and Cognition at the $1B+ tier proves the market is real and growing. More enterprise AI agent deployments = more need for governance, audit trails, and compliance layers — Alizé's core offering.

2. **Factory's multi-model switching is architecturally similar to Alizé's governance layer.** Factory doesn't commit to a single LLM — it routes between models. Alizé's governance layer sits above this routing logic and provides audit trails, compliance, and oversight regardless of which model is used.

3. **Blackstone as an investor is a PE signal.** Blackstone (private equity, not traditional VC) investing in Factory aligns with the broader structural shift toward PE driving large AI deals (80% of deals >€50M are PE-driven, per last week's pulse). This reinforces Alizé's PE-focused go-to-market strategy.

4. **Morgan Stanley, EY, Palo Alto Networks as customers validate enterprise AI for regulated industries.** Financial services, professional services, and cybersecurity all have strong compliance requirements — exactly the customer profile where Alizé's governance layer creates value.

5. **Factory's $1.5B valuation at $150M raised implies ~10x revenue multiple or strong growth expectations.** This sets a high benchmark for AI agent companies raising in 2026.
