## Signal 1: Claude Cowork — Anthropic's "AI That Acts For You" Goes Live

Anthropic launched Claude Cowork (March 24, 2026), a major upgrade to its professional tier that gives Claude full control of your computer: opening apps, navigating the web, clicking, typing, filling spreadsheets, sending emails — autonomously.

**Key facts:**
- Orange visual halo signals when Claude has mouse/keyboard control
- Tasks can be assigned from phone and completed on your desktop while you're away
- Available on Mac, professional tier only (€20-100/month)
- Reliability still imperfect for complex tasks
- Anthropic warns against exposing sensitive data (passwords, banking, medical info)
- Anthropic explicitly recommends human validation for important actions

**What this means for Alizé:**
The AI agent paradigm has shifted again. Claude no longer advises — it acts. This is Anthropic's direct answer to Microsoft's Copilot Cowork (March 30). The "AI coworker" battle is now between Anthropic and Microsoft, with both treating it as a premium enterprise feature.

The implication for Alizé: the market is being conditioned to expect AI that works *inside* their business tools, not just answers questions. French PME will soon ask "why can't our AI do what Claude Cowork does in our Sage/Cegid/Pennylane?" That's Alizé's opening — generic AI coworker is the demo, vertical AI coworker is the product.

**Also notable:** A Coface/Observatoire study cited by FranceInfo projects 5 million jobs lost in France by 2030 due to generative AI — particularly administrative, accounting, legal, and information roles. 16% of the French labor market is "at risk." This is the labor market Alizé's target customers operate in.

## Signal 2: Claude Mythos — Anthropic's Unreleased "Capybara" Model Leaked

Documents briefly accessible on Anthropic's site (Fortune, March 26) revealed "Claude Mythos" — internally described as the most powerful model Anthropic has ever built, with internal codename Capybara.

**Key facts:**
- Outperforms Claude Opus 4.6 significantly on coding and complex reasoning
- Capable of identifying and exploiting software vulnerabilities at speed exceeding most defensive capabilities
- Internal engineers are concerned about cyber offense capabilities
- Deployment likely restricted to cyber defense specialists initially
- Anthropic currently in dispute with Pentagon over military use of Claude
- A federal judge recently blocked a directive restricting Claude use in some US federal agencies

**What this means for Alizé:**
Two things are relevant:
1. The AI capability frontier continues to advance rapidly — Alizé's "governance and supervision" positioning becomes more critical as agents become more capable (and more dangerous if misconfigured)
2. Anthropic is willing to fight regulators (Pentagon dispute) to preserve its deployment principles — this positions them as the "safe AI" player, which aligns with Alizé's positioning around controlled, supervised deployment

## Signal 3: Kestra — French Orchestration Platform Raises €21M Series A

Kestra (Paris-based, founded at Leroy Merlin) raised €21M Series A (March 31, 2026), led by RTP Global with Alven, ISAI, Axeleo participating. Total raised: $36M.

**Key facts:**
- Open-source orchestration platform for complex workflows (data + infrastructure + AI + business operations)
- 25x revenue growth in 18 months
- 2 billion workflows executed in 2025 (20x growth YoY)
- Clients include JPMorgan, Toyota, Deutsche Telekom, Bloomberg, **Crédit Agricole**
- Launching cloud managed offering + Kestra 2.0 (new distributed execution engine)
- Plan to double headcount from 50 to 100

**What this means for Alizé:**
Kestra is the most relevant French company to Alizé's orchestration layer. Key observations:

1. **Crédit Agricole is already using Kestra** — Crédit Agricole is a target customer for Alizé. If they're already automating with Kestra, they may already have internal orchestration competency that Alizé can complement (not replace) with AI agent deployment and governance.

2. **Orchestration is becoming the central nervous system** of enterprise AI — Kestra's 20x workflow growth reflects this. Alizé should consider whether integration with Kestra (rather than competition) makes sense.

3. **Open source + enterprise is the proven go-to-market** for French infrastructure — Kestra started open source at Leroy Merlin, reached enterprise clients, then raised. This mirrors the path Alizé could take for its connector infrastructure.

4. **Kestra's positioning is "orchestration for complex workflows"** — Alizé's positioning should be differentiated: Kestra handles *how* workflows run, Alizé handles *what* the AI agent does inside those workflows.

## Immediate Actions for Alizé

1. **Update positioning to "vertical AI coworker for French PME"** — The generic "AI coworker" is now being owned by Anthropic and Microsoft. Alizé's differentiation is *context*: we deploy AI coworkers that know French accounting (Sage, Cegid, Pennylane), French HR (Kelio, Lucca), and French business workflows. Claude Cowork is the demo; Alizé is the deployment.

2. **Explore Kestra integration** — Crédit Agricole uses Kestra. If Alizé's AI agents need to orchestrate with complex enterprise workflows, Kestra's 1,200+ plugin ecosystem is a natural integration layer. Not a competitor — a force multiplier.

3. **Domain remains the #1 blocker** — alize.ai or alize.fr. This has been blocking the Alizé brand for 3+ weeks. Louis needs to decide today.

4. **Build one MCP connector this week** — The MCP ecosystem is growing (n8n, Claude, Manus). Alizé's connector moat is the French business tool stack. Sage or Cegid as the first connector changes the conversation from "another AI company" to "the company that actually connects to French business software."
