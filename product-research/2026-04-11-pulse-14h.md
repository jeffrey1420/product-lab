## Product Direction Recommendations

1. **Use Anthropic's maturity as social proof**: The $30B revenue milestone + Google partnership = Anthropic is enterprise-safe. Reference this in Alizé's technical architecture documentation.

2. **Build the "AI provenance" checklist**: With Chinese distillation concerns and the US Claude ban, French enterprise clients will ask "where does our data go?" Alizé should have a one-pager on model selection criteria including jurisdiction, data residency, and compliance.

3. **Adopt Mistral as the European alternative**: Given geopolitical tensions with US AI providers, Alizé should architect its solutions with Mistral as the primary model for French clients, Claude as the premium option.

4. **Position Alizé as the "French SME-friendly OpenAI Frontier"**: OpenAI's enterprise platform is too complex and expensive for PME. Alizé is the equivalent for companies that can't hire McKinsey + OpenAI to deploy.

5. **Study OpenClaude's multi-provider architecture**: OpenClaude's provider-agnostic approach is the right model for Alizé's technical stack. Build flexibility to swap models based on client requirements and geopolitical considerations.

6. **Leverage Claude Opus 4.6 plan mode**: Anthropic's governance features (mandatory human review) align perfectly with Alizé's positioning. Architect client workflows to use these features.

7. **Create GEO content targeting French decision-makers**: With Google publishing AI agent trend reports, French business media will amplify AI agent messaging. Alizé should have French-language content ready for when that wave hits.
