## Signal 1: Salesforce Positions Slack as the Enterprise AI Agent Interface

On April 2, 2026, Salesforce began actively positioning Slack as the interface layer for enterprise AI agents. Slackbot is being promoted as a Model Context Protocol (MCP) client that routes queries and tasks to both in-house and third-party enterprise applications, with direct linkage to Salesforce Agentforce.

**What this means:**
- The "agent interface" battle is being won by Slack/Microsoft. Every major enterprise AI platform will need a Slack strategy or an MCP connector.
- MCP is no longer a developer curiosity — it's becoming enterprise infrastructure. Every AI agent platform needs to be MCP-native to survive.
- The corporate workspace (Slack, Teams) is becoming the de facto control plane for AI agents in the enterprise.

**Action for Alizé:** Alizé's agents need to be MCP-native and ideally Slack-compatible. Louis should prioritize building an MCP connector strategy. The Alizé "control plane" for PME should either integrate with Slack or offer a comparably lightweight interface.

## Signal 2: HCLTech AI Force 2.0 — Major IT Services Player Enters the Agentic AI Platform Space

HCLTech (global IT services, $14.5B revenue, 226k people) launched AI Force 2.0 on April 1, 2026 — a model-agnostic modular platform unifying engineering, operations, and applications within a single "intelligence fabric." The platform embeds Responsible AI evaluators for governance and auditability.

**What this means:**
- The global IT services giants (Accenture, HCLTech, TCS, Infosys) are rapidly building proprietary agentic AI platforms. This validates the market but also raises the competitive bar for pure-play startups like Alizé.
- "Governance, security, measurable ROI" language directly mirrors Alizé's positioning. This message is becoming table stakes, not differentiator.
- Enterprise buyers will have a choice: expensive global SI (HCLTech, Accenture) vs. focused French SME-focused managed agent provider (Alizé). The wedge is price + localization + relationship.

**Alizé takeaway:** Don't try to out-feature HCLTech. Double down on what SIs can't offer PME: French-language support, fast deployment, no 6-month onboarding, and a direct relationship.

## Signal 3: Omniscient (Paris) Raises €3.5M Pre-Seed — Multi-Agent Architecture for C-Suite Decision Intelligence

Paris-based Omniscient (founded by two McKinsey alumni) raised €3.5M ($4.1M) pre-Seed from Seedcamp, Drysdale, Plug and Play, Raise, and others. The platform provides C-suite decision intelligence by deploying proprietary specialist AI agents — each covering a defined domain (reputation, regulation, supply chain, competition) — scanning 100k+ sources and synthesizing outputs into a 2-minute executive recap via a management cockpit.

Key claims: legacy intelligence methods are 50x slower; the platform operates across multiple markets, languages, and strategic topics simultaneously.

**Why this matters for Alizé:**
- The specialist multi-agent architecture is validated: domain-specific agents feeding a synthesis layer. This is exactly what Alizé's back-office agents could do for PME operations.
- Omniscient targets boards and executives — the same C-suite that approves Alizé budgets. The economic argument is identical: cost of missed signals/opportunities is enormous.
- McKinsey alumni building vertical agent businesses = smart, well-networked competitors. Watch this space.

**Alizé takeaway:** Omniscient proves the multi-agent synthesis model works for high-level decision support. Alizé can apply the same architecture to operational PME workflows — lower stakes, higher volume.

## Signal 4: Ludocare Becomes First French Digital Therapy to Receive HAS Reimbursement

On April 2, 2026, the Haute Autorité de Santé (HAS) issued a favorable opinion for reimbursement of Ludocare's Joe — a pediatric digital therapy (DTx) for asthma in children. This is the first time a French digital therapy has been recommended for Social Security reimbursement. Ludocare demonstrated a 42% reduction in serious complications.

**Why this matters for Alizé:**
- France is willing to reimburse AI-powered solutions when clinical evidence is strong. This sets a precedent.
- The regulatory pathway for AI products in France is becoming clearer. Alizé should track HAS and DSRP (dispositif spécifique de recherche professionnelle) frameworks for AI agent governance.
- Health/medtech AI is leading the reimbursement narrative, which will eventually extend to enterprise AI agents in regulated industries.
