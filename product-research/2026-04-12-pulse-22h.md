## 1. Forrester 2026 B2B Predictions — Key Findings for Alize

Source: Forrester B2B Marketing, Sales & Product Predictions 2026 (Oct 2025 research)

### Finding 1: AI-Enabled Buyers Are Less Confident, Not More

**19% of buyers using genAI tools feel less confident in their purchasing decisions** due to inaccurate or unreliable information. Despite vendors pushing AI everywhere, buyers are increasingly skeptical of AI-generated content and recommendations.

**Implication for Alize:** This is a validation of the "human-in-the-loop" positioning. If bare genAI tools leave buyers uncertain, a managed service with human oversight, governance, and accountability addresses the real problem. Lead with the human element, not the AI.

**Quote framing:** "L'IA seule vous laisse avec des doutes. Nous, on vous laisse avec des résultats vérifiés."

---

### Finding 2: Human Expertise Is Making a Comeback as a Differentiator

In 2025, 30% of buyers viewed genAI tools as a meaningful interaction type during final commitment. But buyers are now turning to **human experts to validate AI-generated insights** — especially for complex decisions.

**Implication for Alize:** The "internal champion" persona (from yesterday's personas) needs peer/ expert validation to sell AI internally. Alize should build in a human expert handoff — not just dashboard screenshots.

**Channel insight:** Analyst reports and expert content are among the most meaningful touchpoints for B2B buyers. Alize should produce one credible piece of content (PDF or landing page section) that a department head can share with their CEO to justify the investment.

---

### Finding 3: Agent-vs-Agent B2B Negotiations Are Emerging

**61% of purchase influencers now use a private genAI engine to support purchasing decisions.** In 2026, at least 1 in 5 B2B sellers will be forced to respond to AI-powered buyer agents with dynamically delivered counteroffers via seller-controlled agents.

**Implication for Alize:** This is a future signal, not immediate. It means:
- Buyer research is increasingly done by AI agents (Alize needs to be findable/optimized for AI crawlers)
- In 2-3 years, Alize may need seller-side agents to respond to buyer-side agents
- Short-term: ensure Alize's web presence is clean, factual, and AI-readable (structured data, clear offers)

---

### Finding 4: $10B+ Will Be Lost to Ungoverned GenAI

Forrester estimates that ungoverned genAI use in commercial applications will cost B2B companies over **$10 billion in enterprise value** (declining stock prices, legal settlements, fines) through 2026.

**Implication for Alize:** "Governance" is not a buzzword — it's a financial risk mitigation story. French companies are particularly sensitive to EU AI Act compliance. Alize's managed service framing ("on s'occupe de tout, c'est gouverné") directly addresses this fear.

**Pitch angle:** "Vos équipes utilisent déjà l'IA non gouvernée — c'est un risque. Nous on livre l'IA avec le cadre."

---

### Finding 5: Influencer Relations Budgets Are Surging

75% of enterprise B2B companies will increase budgets for influencer relations. Buying networks are expanding and buyers increasingly rely on external influencers — analysts, subject matter experts, and industry voices — for fact-based insights.

**Implication for Alize:** In the French PME market, "influencers" are not TikTok personalities — they're:
- Bpifrance (government agency) endorsements
- Industry associations ( CPME, MEDEF)
- Peer recommendations from other CEOs
- Trusted consultants

Alize should pursue Bpifrance ecosystem presence and aim for 2-3 reference clients who will vouch for them publicly.

## 2. AI Transformation in B2B — Key Statistics

Source: Mondaq "Complete Guide to AI Transformation in B2B Companies in 2026"

### AI Adoption in Sales Workflows
- **By 2027, 95% of B2B seller research workflows will begin with AI** (up from <20% in 2024) — Gartner
- AI agents now handle: lead qualification, meeting scheduling, product Q&A, and negotiating terms within defined parameters
- Conversational AI agents engage website visitors 24/7, qualifying prospects before human handoff

**Implication for Alize:** The market is moving toward AI-first buying. This is a volume argument — more buyers will be AI-sophisticated and expect AI-level service. But the trust deficit (Forrester) means the human validation layer remains critical.

### Outcome-Based Models Are Emerging
Companies are increasingly selling **outcomes instead of products** — e.g., industrial firms guaranteeing machine uptime, shifting from selling equipment to selling performance.

**Implication for Alize:** This directly validates the Quick Win offer design from the prior pulse: "1 Workflow, 4 Weeks, Preuve de ROI." If Alize can guarantee an outcome (X% time saved, Y leads enriched), they can move upmarket from software licensing to outcome contracts.

## 4. B2B Buyer Behavior: The French PME Specifics

Synthesizing from Bpifrance data in prior pulses + Forrester findings:

### Buying Process for French PME AI Purchases

| Stage | Duration | Key Activity | Alize Touchpoint |
|-------|----------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Awareness** | Ongoing | CEO hears about AI from peers, LinkedIn, media | Content, peer network |
| **Discovery** | 1-4 weeks | Google, ask peers, vendor demos | Landing page, first call |
| **Evaluation** | 2-6 weeks | Compare options, internal business case | Quick Win offer, case study |
| **Decision** | 1-2 weeks | Budget approval, contract | Proposal, ROI document |
| **Implementation** | 4-8 weeks | Deployment, change management | Alize delivers Quick Win |

### Conversion Drivers for French PME
1. **Peer validation** — "Un concurrent l'utilise et ça marche"
2. **Quick ROI proof** — "En 4 semaines, vous avez vos premiers résultats"
3. **Simplicity** — "On s'occupe de tout"
4. **Data trust** — "Vos données restent en France"
5. **Risk reversal** — "Si ça ne marche pas, on ajuste"

### Top Objections from French PME Buyers
1. "On a déjà essayé, ça n'a rien donné" (past failure)
2. "C'est trop cher pour nous" (budget constraint)
3. "Nos équipes ne vont pas adopter ça" (change resistance)
4. "Où vont nos données ?" (data sovereignty)
5. "Comment on mesure le ROI ?" (accountability gap)

## 5. Alize Sales Cycle Estimate

- **Best case:** 4-6 weeks (decision to signature)
