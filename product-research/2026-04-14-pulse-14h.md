## Signal 1: Dassault Systèmes x10 AI Companions — Full Deep-Dive

**Context:** This is the planned focus from the 10:00 UTC pulse. DS announced at 3DExperience World 2026 (Houston, February) a partnership with NVIDIA to embed AI agents ("compagnons IA") directly into 3DEXPERIENCE.

**What was confirmed and expanded:**

**The x10 productivity claim:**
Pascal Daloz (CEO of Dassault Systèmes) stated on stage that the goal is not incremental speed improvement — it is 10x productivity through three vectors:
1. Automating repetitive tasks (model prep, data reconciliation, documentation)
2. Enabling more design iterations (test more variants, explore more concepts)
3. Reducing time to validation and certification

**Three AI companions, covering all of 3DEXPERIENCE portfolio:**
- **CAD/CAO** — Companions embedded in the design environment
- **SIMULIA simulation** — Physics-validated AI companions for simulation setup and analysis
- **DELMIA production** — AI companions for manufacturing, production planning, and factory simulation
- **PLM lifecycle** — Cross-functional companions navigating requirements, specifications, and change history

**The architectural model — key for Alize:**

DS describes agents that:
- Live *inside* the workflow, not as a chatbot alongside it
- Understand industrial objects: geometries, meshes, parameters, requirements, versions, dependencies
- Can reason about a graph of dependencies (change one dimension → cascades through tolerance, process, QC)
- Must be able to explain their reasoning (traceability requirement for aerospace/defense)

This is exactly the "governed AI agent" architecture that Alize is positioning around. The difference: DS agents operate at the engineering/design layer; Alize agents operate at the back-office/business process layer. But the governance model (explainability, traceability, human-in-loop) is identical.

**The OUTSCALE sovereign cloud play:**
- DS, through its OUTSCALE subsidiary, is deploying AI factories on three continents with NVIDIA infrastructure
- This is French sovereign AI infrastructure for industrial customers
- Data privacy and IP protection are explicit selling points — relevant for French defense/aerospace companies
- Alize angle: if French manufacturers trust DS + OUTSCALE with their engineering AI, Alize can position itself as the layer connecting DS-embedded agents to business workflows (ERP, CRM, HR systems) that DS does not touch.

**World Models validated scientifically:**
- DS and NVIDIA are building "Industry World Models" — domain-specific AI trained on physics-validated industrial data
- These are NOT general-purpose LLMs. They are models grounded in real physical laws and industrial constraints.
- This is the model Alize should use in its arguments: generic AI fails in industrial contexts because it doesn't understand physical constraints; Alize-powered agents can bridge generic AI capabilities with domain-specific validation.

**The human oversight guardrail:**
Pascal Daloz explicitly stated: "if you don't understand your model, your material, your load hypothesis, AI will just help you go faster in the wrong direction." Physics and fundamentals remain essential.

This is the exact Alize message: AI agents that work *with* engineers, not instead of them. Alize can take this DS narrative and apply it to back-office: AI agents that work *with* operations teams, not replace them.

**Alize positioning relative to DS:**

| DS layer | Alize layer | Connection |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| 3DEXPERIENCE design/engineering agents | Back-office agents (HR, finance, ops) | DS agents output → Alize agents process results, trigger downstream workflows |
| DELMIA production agents | Procurement, supply chain | Alize agents orchestrate procurement based on DELMIA-driven production signals |
| OUTSCALE sovereign infrastructure | Alize governance + deployment | Alize manages the agent layer on top of sovereign infrastructure |
| BIOVIA research agents | R&D admin, compliance | Alize agents handle R&D reporting and regulatory compliance |

**The critical insight:** DS is building the engineering AI layer. Alize should position as the business operations AI layer that connects to DS outputs. French manufacturers using 3DEXPERIENCE will still have:
- HR processes (onboarding, payroll)
- Finance processes (invoicing, reporting, treasury)
- Procurement processes ( purchasing, supplier management)
- Compliance and legal processes

Alize agents own these. The DS partnership with NVIDIA validates the architecture; Alize rides that wave.

## Signal 3: Inria Report on Agentic AI (March 23, 2026)

The French national research institute (Inria) published a major report framing agentic AI as "the next turning point in artificial intelligence." Key points:

- Agentic AI systems can now **plan, decide, and act** autonomously, not just respond to queries
- Enterprise adoption is accelerating beyond chatbots into autonomous workflow execution
- The challenge is no longer capability — it is **deployment, governance, and trust**
- Inria identifies three gaps: reliability (hallucination in autonomous agents), security (agent actions at scale), and human oversight (keeping humans in the loop without defeating the purpose)

**This is Alize's core sales pitch, confirmed by France's premier AI research institute.** The framing "agentique, pas替代" — agents that augment, not replace — is the consensus French position.
