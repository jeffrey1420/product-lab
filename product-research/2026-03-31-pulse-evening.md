## Signal 2: NIST AI Governance Standards — AI Agents Enter Federal Oversight

**NIST released federal standards initiatives for autonomous AI systems in 2026.** The transition from assisted copilots to fully autonomous operational agents has triggered federal-level governance attention. NIST's framework covers:
- Traceability and audit trails for agent actions
- Data access controls and scope limitation
- Human-in-the-loop checkpoints for high-stakes actions
- Incident response and rollback procedures

**Why this matters for Alizé:** French companies working with EU data already face GDPR. Adding NIST-aligned governance documentation (even without US exposure) signals enterprise-grade rigor. Alizé's "opérationnel avec supervision" positioning maps directly to NIST's traceability and human-in-the-loop requirements.

**Action item:** Add a "NIST-aligned governance" section to Alizé's pilot documentation — even if clients don't ask for it, it differentiates from competitors offering ungoverned agent access.

## Signal 4: AI Agent Pricing Models — From Per-Seat to Outcome-Based

**The AI agent pricing landscape is fragmenting across three models:**

| Model | Example | Pros | Cons |
|-------|---------|------|------|
| **Per-seat** | Manus, most platforms | Simple, predictable | Hard to scale, may overprovision |
| **Per-task/transaction** | Agentova, some CX tools | Aligns cost to value | Hard to predict spend |
| **Outcome-based (retainer)** | Emerging enterprise | Aligns vendor to results | Complex to define metrics |

**Alizé pricing implication:** For a managed service model targeting PME/ETI, a monthly retainer + success metrics model is the strongest fit. It signals confidence ("I bet on my agents delivering results") and creates recurring revenue. Pilot pricing should anchor to this model: flat monthly fee, defined KPIs, review at 30/60/90 days.
