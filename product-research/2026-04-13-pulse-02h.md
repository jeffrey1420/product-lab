## 1. B2B Buyer Behavior: The Full Journey Map

### 1a. The 60-70% Silent Journey

The most important structural fact about B2B buying in 2026: **60-70% of the buying journey is complete before a human salesperson enters the picture.** Buyers have delegated their research to private AI agents rather than Google searches.

Source: Smart Team "B2B Sales in 2026: The Era of Agentic AI"

**Implication for Alize:** The first human conversation happens late — after the buyer has already formed opinions. By the time Alize gets a first call, the prospect has likely already:
- Narrowed down to 2-3 options
- Built an internal business case
- Identified a champion

This means Alize's landing page and first-call content must be optimized for **AI agent readability** — structured data, clear value propositions, no buzzword dilution. If an AI agent can't find and recommend Alize, the prospect may never reach out.

**Action:** Audit the Alize landing page for AI-crawler readability. Structured markup, clear service definition, outcome-focused language.

### 1b. The B2B Buying Journey: 5 Stages

| Stage | Duration | What Happens | Alize's Job |
|-------|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Awareness** | Ongoing | CEO hears AI from peers, LinkedIn, media | Be present in French AI discourse |
| **Discovery** | 1-4 weeks | Google/AI agent search, peer questions | Landing page + peer network |
| **Evaluation** | 2-6 weeks | Compare options, internal business case | Quick Win offer, ROI doc |
| **Decision** | 1-2 weeks | Budget approval, contract signature | Proposal + trust signals |
| **Implementation** | 4-8 weeks | Deployment, change management | Alize delivers — this is the product |

**Key insight:** The "sales process" is only 1-4 weeks but the evaluation stage is 2-6 weeks. Most of the buyer's time is spent **internally**, not with vendors. This means:

1. Alize needs strong internal champion activation materials (ROI calculator, one-pager they can share with their CEO)
2. The business case happens without Alize in the room — give champions ammunition

## 2. Sales Qualification Frameworks: BANT vs MEDDIC

### When to Use BANT (Fast Triage)

BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline) is the right tool when:
- High-volume inbound leads
- Transactional sales under €25K
- SMB buyers who can decide without committee approval
- Quick first call screening

**BANT applied to Alize Quick Win:**
- **Budget:** €10K-20K for Quick Win — most 50-200 person companies can find this
- **Authority:** CEO or Dirigeant is the decision-maker in PME — often same person as champion
- **Need:** Must have a specific, named repetitive task — not "on veut faire IA"
- **Timeline:** Must be "imminent" or "this quarter" — not a 6-month strategic plan

### When to Use MEDDIC (Complex Deals)

MEDDIC (Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion) is for:
- Monthly retainer deals €3K-5K/month
- Companies with formal purchasing processes
- Multi-stakeholder buying committees
- Enterprise/ETI targets

**MEDDIC applied to Alize Monthly Retainer:**
- **Metrics:** "Combien d'heures/mois passent vos équipes sur des tâches répétitives ?"
- **Economic Buyer:** Identified during first call — CFO or CEO in PME
- **Decision Criteria:** ROI proof, data security, simplicity
- **Decision Process:** Is there a formal purchasing process? Who signs?
- **Identify Pain:** Quantified pain — X hours/week lost to Y task
- **Champion:** Who inside the company will advocate for Alize?

**Implication for Alize:** Start every first call with BANT-style fast qualification. If it passes, transition to MEDDIC depth for the second call. Never try to MEDDIC-qualify on the first call with a busy PME owner.

## 3. The Champion-Driven Sale

**Key finding from the Smart Team article:** In 2026, the "champion" inside the buying company is the most critical asset in a complex sale. Without an internal advocate, AI agent recommendations alone won't close deals.

**Champion's job in the Alize sale:**
1. Convince their CEO it's worth trying
2. Identify the first workflow to automate
3. Manage internal resistance ("nos équipes ne vont pas adopter ça")
4. Report results back to Alize for case study

**Alize's job to support the champion:**
1. Give them a one-pager they can show their CEO (peer-shareable, credible)
2. Give them ROI calculation tools
3. Make the Quick Win look low-risk ("si ça ne marche pas, on ajuste")
4. Celebrate their success publicly (with permission) — they need visibility

**Warning:** Without a champion, Alize is just another vendor in an inbox. The entire sales motion for PME is champion-activated.

## 5. What's Different This Week: New Signals

### Signal: "Agent-Ready" Is Becoming a B2B Requirement

By 2026, buyers are asking AI agents to do their vendor research. This means companies need to be **"Agent-Ready"** — discoverable, readable, and recommendable by AI systems. 

**Definition of an Agent-Ready company:**
1. Has a clear, structured service description (not marketing fluff)
2. Publishes outcomes and pricing (even indicative ranges)
3. Has verifiable social proof (case studies, reviews)
4. Is crawlable by AI agents (clear schema markup)

**Alize action:** Audit the landing page from an AI-agent perspective. If a buying AI agent is researching "managed AI agents for French PME," would Alize come up in the top 3 recommendations?

### Signal: B2B Buyers Trust Human Experts More Than GenAI at Decision Stage

From the Smart Team data: At the final commitment stage, 30% of buyers view genAI tools as meaningful interactions. But this number is **declining** as buyers realize AI-generated recommendations are unreliable.

Human expert validation is making a comeback — 17% of buyers find product expert interactions meaningful at final commitment, and this number is **increasing**.

**Implication for Alize:** The Alize sales motion should always end with a human expert moment — a call, a concrete demo of a real agent, a site visit. Don't let the deal be closed by a chatbot or email sequence.

## 6. Alize Sales Motion: Recommended Framework

Based on this pulse's B2B buyer behavior research, here's the recommended Alize sales motion for Quick Win deals:

### Week 1: Qualify (BANT Fast Triage)
- **Cold outreach** via email to identified champion
- 15-min discovery call: is this a real need, do they have budget, can they decide
- If BANT passes → schedule Strategy Call

### Week 2: Qualify Deep (MEDDIC Light)
- 45-min Strategy Call: map the pain, identify the workflow, introduce Quick Win
- Give champion: ROI worksheet + one-pager to share with CEO
- If aligned → send Quick Win proposal

### Week 3-4: Proposal + Close
- Proposal: "1 workflow, 4 weeks, €10K-15K, ROI proof or we adjust"
- 1 call to close — champion presents to CEO with Alize support
- Signature + payment

### Week 5-8: Delivery
- Alize deploys first workflow
- Weekly check-in with champion
- End of Week 8: ROI review + retainer conversation

**Total: ~10 weeks from first email to retained client**
