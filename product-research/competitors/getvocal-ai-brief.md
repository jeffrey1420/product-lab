## Company Overview

**GetVocal AI** is a Paris-based startup developing hybrid human-AI agents for enterprise customer experience (CX). Their platform automates customer support conversations while maintaining human oversight for complex or sensitive cases.

**Founded:** Not confirmed in public sources, likely 2020-2022
**HQ:** Paris, France
**Funding:** ~€25M total (€22M Series A, November 2025)
- **Lead investor:** Creandum (early Spotify backer)
- **Participating investors:** Elaia, Speedinvest
- **Notable:** Tier-1 European investors, not just French

**Key people:** Not publicly named in early coverage.

## Product

### Core Offering

GetVocal's product is a **hybrid workforce platform** for customer support. It is NOT:
- A chatbot vendor
- A simple FAQ automation tool
- A self-service LLM wrapper

It IS:
- A managed fleet of AI agents that handle customer conversations autonomously
- A real-time dashboard for human supervisors to monitor, intervene, and optimize
- A system where AI and human agents work together just as junior and senior human agents do

### Architecture: The "Context Graph"

The core differentiator is what GetVocal calls a **Context Graph** — described as combining:
- The natural fluency of LLMs (generative AI)
- The precision of deterministic rule enforcement (structured logic)

This is architecturally similar to what Alizé should build as its "governance layer." The insight is the same: pure LLMs are insufficient for enterprise-grade reliability; you need structured constraints layered on top.

### Key Product Features

1. **Agent Builder:** Balance deterministic AI and generative AI for specific use cases
2. **Agent Control Center:** Real-time monitoring + human intervention capability
3. **Hybrid Workforce Platform:** Manage all human + AI agents in one interface
4. **Science-Driven Agent Learning:** Agents improve over time based on outcomes

### Operating Model Options

GetVocal offers three go-to-market paths:
1. **Self-serve:** Customer builds agents using GetVocal's builder and templates
2. **Template-based:** Faster deployment using pre-built industry templates
3. **Turnkey (Agent Deployment Team):** GetVocal's team deploys and configures for the customer

*This turnkey model is identical to Alizé's proposed managed service offering — the market has validated this go-to-market motion.*

## Competitive Position vs Alizé

### Where GetVocal Wins

| Dimension | GetVocal | Alizé |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| **Vertical** | Customer experience / support | Back-office operations (finance, HR, logistics admin) |
| **Segment** | Mid-to-large enterprise | PME / ETI (50-250 employees) |
| **Funding** | €22M Series A | Pre-revenue, no institutional funding |
| **Market traction** | Live customers, enterprise contracts | Building/productizing |
| **Go-to-market** | Sales-led, direct | Likely outbound/prospecting-led |

### Where Alizé Has Advantage

1. **Vertical breadth:** GetVocal is CX-only. Alizé can operate across finance ops, HR workflows, logistics, legal admin — no single vertical dependency.
2. **Smaller segment:** 50-250 employee companies are underserved by enterprise CX platforms (too small for GetVocal's deal size). This is Alizé's sweet spot.
3. **Sovereign AI angle:** GetVocal likely uses GPT-4/Claude. Alizé can differentiate on Mistral AI + French data residency + GDPR compliance.
4. **Outcome-based pricing:** If Alizé prices per workflow outcome vs per-seat, it aligns incentives differently from GetVocal's likely per-conversation pricing.
5. **No VivaTech main stage:** GetVocal has the platform stage; Alizé needs to find its own venue to build credibility.

### Threat Assessment

| Timeframe | Threat Level | Rationale |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Short-term (0-6 months) | **LOW** | Different vertical, different segment. No direct competition. |
| Medium-term (6-18 months) | **MEDIUM** | GetVocal could expand to back-office if they see traction. They have the architecture to do it. |
| Long-term (18+ months) | **HIGH** | If GetVocal raises a Series B (likely given their trajectory), they could move into operational workflows. |

**Overall:** GetVocal is NOT an immediate Alizé competitor. They are validation that the hybrid agent managed service model works in France with French enterprise clients. They are the benchmark for what a well-funded French AI agent company looks like.

## Key Takeaways for Alizé

1. **The Context Graph model (governance + LLM) is validated.** GetVocal proves this architecture works commercially. Alizé should explicitly describe its governance layer in similar terms.

2. **The turnkey managed service go-to-market is validated.** GetVocal's Agent Deployment Team offering is the same model Alizé proposes. This is not a unique insight — it is the right model.

3. **French enterprise AI appetite is real.** GetVocal raised €22M from tier-1 European investors (Creandum). French companies are buying managed AI agent services.

4. **Alizé needs a different battlefield.** Trying to compete with GetVocal in CX would be suicide. Alizé's differentiator is operational back-office (not customer-facing), smaller companies, and sovereign AI positioning.

5. **GetVocal is a useful competitive reference.** In sales conversations, Alizé can say: "You may have seen GetVocal for customer support — we do the same thing for your back-office operations." Name recognition helps.
