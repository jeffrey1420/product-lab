## Signal 1: Belitsoft — Average Enterprise Runs 12 AI Agents, Half Work in Isolation

Belitsoft published its 2026 AI Agent Trends report on April 6, finding that enterprises now run an average of **12 AI agents** concurrently. The critical finding: roughly half of these agents operate in isolation — without interacting with other agents, shared data sources, or centralized oversight.

This means the average enterprise has a growing constellation of disconnected AI workers, not an integrated AI workforce. No supervisor, no shared context, no coordination layer. This is a direct market signal: someone needs to be the connective tissue.

**What this means for Alizé:**
The "12 agents, half isolated" finding is the multi-agent orchestration gap in plain English. Alizé's managed service model is not just about deploying agents — it's about deploying a *coordinated system* of agents that share context, follow governance rules, and report to a human supervisor. The isolated agents are the problem; Alizé is the solution. This data point is directly usable in sales conversations: "Your company probably has 12 agents running right now. Ours work together."

The pattern is consistent: governance and orchestration tools are not overhead. They are the infrastructure that makes production deployment possible.

**What this means for Alizé:**
"Governance tools deliver 12x more production deployments" is the single most quotable stat for Alizé's positioning. It inverts the assumption that governance slows you down. For a French PME that wants to get AI agents into production without the risk of going off-rails, Alizé's governance layer is the reason they succeed — not the bureaucratic hurdle they have to clear.

## Signal 3: Deloitte 2026 — SaaS Becomes Agentic, Pricing and Sales Models Under Pressure

Deloitte's "SaaS meets AI agents" 2026 prediction outlines how enterprise software will fundamentally change:

**Pricing shift:** The current per-seat SaaS subscription model is structurally misaligned with autonomous AI agents. Outcome-based and value-based pricing are emerging — paying based on business results produced (tickets resolved, revenue influenced, employees hired). Deloitte notes this requires new contractual frameworks: definitions of "an agent," "a task," "an outcome," and how value is attributed. Multi-vendor multi-agent environments make this exponentially harder.

**The "control center" layer:** Deloitte predicts significant competition around the agent interaction layer — stand-alone apps, vendor-controlled gateways, and third-party management platforms that integrate agents from multiple vendors and track usage, spend, access, performance, security, and compliance in one place. This is essentially what Alizé is building.

**UI evolution:** Agent interfaces will become personalized, conversational (natural language replacing menus and clicks), and diagnostic (explainability and audit trails for when things go wrong). The interface shifts from "telling software what to do" to "asking software to achieve an outcome."

**Sales model disruption:** SaaS vendors will need to educate buyers, change compensation models, and build deeper customer relationships as pricing shifts from subscriptions to outcomes.

**What this means for Alizé:**
Deloitte's "control center" prediction validates Alizé's positioning three times over. The market is converging on the need for a neutral, multi-vendor orchestration and governance layer. The pricing confusion (outcome-based vs. subscription) is actually an opportunity — Alizé can help clients navigate it with clear SLA frameworks rather than trying to define "value" on a per-agent, per-outcome basis for every vendor.

## Signal 5: Google Cloud AI Agent Trends 2026 — Autonomy Levels Framework, Agent Marketplaces Emerging

Google Cloud published its 2026 AI Agent Trends report, introducing (or popularizing) an autonomy levels framework for AI agents — ranging from fully human-guided to fully autonomous. The report also identifies:

- **Agent marketplaces** as the next distribution layer — internal and external agents discoverable and integrable dynamically
- **Vibe coding driving database architecture overhaul** — AI-driven development changing how companies build and scale data infrastructure
- **AI agents creating 80% of all databases and 97% of database branches** on Neon serverless Postgres (acquired by Databricks)

The autonomy framework is notable because it gives enterprises a vocabulary for the governance conversation: "what level of autonomy is appropriate for this agent in our environment?" This is exactly the governance question Alizé needs to answer for clients.

**What this means for Alizé:**
The autonomy levels framework gives Alizé a sales tool: a diagnostic conversation starter. "What level of autonomy should your customer service agent have? What about your regulatory reporting agent?" This maps directly to Alizé's governance and SLA framework. The agent marketplace trend also reinforces the multi-vendor reality that makes Alizé's neutral orchestration role necessary.
