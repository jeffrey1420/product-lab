## Signal 5: "AI May Be Making Us Think and Write More Alike" — Cultural Backlash Signal

A USC Dornsife study flagged in a 106-point HN thread. The cultural conversation around AI homogenization of thought is reaching mainstream awareness. Key HN thread reactions:
- "LLMs will certainly have an effect on how humans reason and communicate"
- "The internet didn't follow the 'fad fades' trajectory. Neither did smartphones."
- "As long as Elon Musk, Sam Altman, Mark Zuckerberg are trying to bend the world to their will... AI will be zeitgeist"

The more dystopian thread: "The development & secrecy of technique will once again become a deep moat" — people will stop sharing knowledge publicly for fear of LLMs absorbing and replicating it at scale.

**What this means for Alizé:**

The homogenization critique is a reputational risk for the AI industry and a potential selling point for Alizé's human-in-the-loop model. If AI is making everyone sound the same and think the same, enterprises that want differentiated thinking will value human-guided AI processes over generic automation.

The "dark forest" scenario — where public information becomes useless because it's all AI-generated — also creates a long-term argument for proprietary, curated AI workflows rather than generic prompt-and-generate AI tools. Alizé's managed service model produces proprietary operational improvements for each client, not generic outputs.
