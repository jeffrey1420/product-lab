## 4. China Bans AI-Generated Code in Software Copyright — Global Precedent

**Source:** [Zhihu / CNIPA Notice](https://www.zhihu.com/question/2022610310935115479) (April 1, 2026)

China's National Copyright Administration (CNIPA) issued new rules:
- Software copyright applications must certify that **no AI was used to generate code or documentation**
- Violators face being added to a **credit record system** (social credit implications)
- Effective immediately as of April 1, 2026

**What this means for Alizé:**
- This is a significant regulatory shift with global precedent implications
- If China does this, the EU may follow with its own AI transparency requirements for software
- French companies (Alizé's target) may face similar disclosure requirements under EU AI Act
- Alizé should build "AI usage disclosure" into its service contracts and documentation process
- This actually creates work for Alizé: helping clients navigate AI disclosure requirements in software
