## Signal 1: Ledger Launches AI Agent Security Layer — Major Validation of Alizé Governance Positioning

**Source:** Maddyness, April 15, 2026

Ledger, the French crypto-hardware unicorn ($4B+ valuation, preparing Wall Street IPO), unveiled a comprehensive AI agent security roadmap. This is the most concrete enterprise-grade AI governance framework seen from a major French tech company — and it maps almost perfectly to what Alizé has been building positioning around.

**The Ledger AI security roadmap (2026):**

- **Q2 2026 — Device-bound agent identity:** AI agents get a hardware-linked identity registered on-chain. Agents are authenticated as corporate devices, not anonymous bots.
- **Q3 2026 — Human-in-the-loop approval system:** Agents operate within defined boundaries (spending caps, usage restrictions, smart contract permissions). Any action outside those boundaries is automatically routed to a human for approval.
- **Q4 2026 — Attestation system:** Every interaction with an AI agent is verified as human-originated, countering bot spam and account multiplication.

**Supporting detail:** Ledger already launched a Device Management Kit (DMK) for developers to embed human-validation mechanisms in agent applications. Ian Rogers (former LVMH digital chief, Apple Music) appointed as Chief Human Agency Officer with mandate: "guarantee humans stay at the center of critical decisions."

**Why this matters for Alizé:**

1. **The governance narrative has gone mainstream.** Ledger — a company with serious security pedigree — is telling French enterprises that AI agents need human oversight, spending limits, and device authentication. Alizé's "workflows with governance" positioning is no longer a contrarian take — it's becoming the default enterprise expectation.

2. **Ledger's IPO will amplify this narrative.** When a $4B+ French company goes to Wall Street with "AI agent security" as a core growth story, US investors will buy the category. French PME/ETI buyers will follow.

3. **"Human Agency Officer" as a job title validates Alizé's service concept.** If large enterprises need a C-suite role dedicated to supervising AI agents, mid-market companies need a managed service to do that work for them. Alizé is precisely that service.

4. **Alizé should reference Ledger in positioning.** In sales conversations, "Ledger figured out that AI agents need hardware-backed identities and human approval gates — we build that governance layer for operational workflows" is a powerful credibility bridge.

## Signal 4: Private Equity Overtakes Venture Capital in European Tech — Implications for B2B Sales

**Source:** Maddyness, April 16, 2026

A structural shift is accelerating: private equity funds now execute **80% of European tech deals over €50M** (vs. 8% in 2006-2010). VC is in structural retreat. Key data:
- Buy-out fundraising down ~16% (vs. VC down 21%) — but PE holds share
- "12 is the new 5": companies need 12% annual EBITDA growth to achieve the multiples previously achieved with 5%
- 80% of large tech deals now PE-backed vs. 8% in 2010

**Why this matters for Alizé:**

1. **Alizé's target clients will increasingly be PE-backed.** A company owned by a PE fund has different priorities than a VC-backed startup: EBITDA optimization, operational efficiency, measurable ROI. Alizé's "workflow with measurable outcome" pitch is perfectly suited for PE-owned companies that need to show operational improvement.

2. **PE-owned companies are more likely to buy managed services.** VC-backed startups try to build everything in-house. PE-owned companies optimize for speed-to-market and operational leverage — exactly where Alizé's managed service model wins.

3. **The "flight to quality" in funding mirrors the "flight to quality" in AI buying.** Just as LPs are moving to PE for risk-adjusted returns, French enterprises are moving from experimental AI (chatbots, pilots) to production AI (structured workflows with governance). Alizé is on the right side of both shifts.
