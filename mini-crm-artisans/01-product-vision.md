# Product Vision — Devis & Factures Tool

## The Pain

French artisans (plumbers, electricians, heating engineers) spend 2-3 hours per week on admin:
- Losing client phone numbers and addresses
- Creating quotes on paper or Word, resending via WhatsApp
- Forgetting to send invoices after a job
- Chasing unpaid invoices manually (awkward)
- Missing follow-ups because there's no system

They hate this admin work. They became artisans to do their trade, not to do paperwork.

## The Product

A daily tool, not a CRM.

**"Je gère mes devis, mes factures, et mes relances — sans y passer ma soirée."**

Four things only:
1. Client file — the address book they lost
2. Quote/devis — create in 2 minutes, send via WhatsApp
3. Invoice/facture — French-legal, PDF, sequential numbering (required by law)
4. Reminder/relance — who hasn't paid, send a nudge

Designed for mobile-first. They do quotes on-site with their phone.

## The Buying Trigger

Not "I need a CRM."

It's: "I just lost Madame Martin's phone number again and I spent 20 minutes looking for it."

Or: "I sent that invoice two weeks ago and I don't want to be the guy chasing payment."

## Why Now

French e-invoicing reform (1 Sept 2026 reception, 1 Sept 2027 emission via approved platforms) means every artisan needs to be able to receive and send legal invoices digitally. This is forcing awareness — they need a solution.

## Competitive Landscape

- Tolteck: €25/mo, 33k users, leads with devis/factures
- Obat: €25-109/mo, 31k users, team access + voice quoting
- These prove the market pays. But both have weaknesses: clunky UX, slow mobile, bloated features.

Our wedge: simplicity, mobile-first, relances (follow-ups they don't have to chase manually).

## Not a CRM

We don't say CRM. We say: "L'outil qui gère vos devis et factures pour vous."

CRM is a category they don't want. Devis/factures/relances is a problem they have.

## Target Customer

**Primary:** Solo heating engineer, electrician, or plumber. 1-2 employees. Smartphone-native. Uses WhatsApp for everything. Loses client details regularly. Has a messy email with 400 unread messages.

**Not our customer:** Jean-Pierre the carpenter with a secretary. Companies with 10+ employees. Traditional paper-first shops.

## What We Don't Do (Yet)

- No Kanban pipeline (imposes project management thinking they don't have)
- No team features (until 20+ paying customers)
- No offline mode (v2 problem)
- No accounting integration (post-launch)
- No inventory (out of scope)

## The Feeling

When it works: "Finally someone built something that understands my job."

When it doesn't: "This is like all the other software — too complicated for me."

We have to be the former.
