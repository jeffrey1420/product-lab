## 1. Poke: AI Agents for Everyone Via iMessage

**Source:** [TechCrunch, April 8, 2026](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/08/poke-makes-ai-agents-as-easy-as-sending-a-text/)

Poke is a consumer AI agent launched by The Interaction Company (Palo Alto, 10 people), accessible via iMessage, SMS, Telegram — no app download required. Text it and it acts on your behalf.

**Key details:**
- **Funding:** $25M total ($15M seed + $10M add-on), backed by Spark Capital, General Catalyst
- **Valuation:** $300M post-money
- **Notable angels:** John & Patrick Collison (Stripe), Logan Kilpatrick (DeepMind), Joanne Jang (OpenAI), Scott Wu & Walden Yan (Cognition), Guillermo Rauch (Vercel), Thomas Wolf (Hugging Face)
- **Pricing:** Free to start; usage-based, roughly $10-30/month for heavier use
- **Model-agnostic:** routes to whichever model fits the task — not locked to one provider
- **Recipes:** user-created automations sharable with others; users earn $0.10-1.00 per referral
- **EU note:** Meta barred rival chatbots from WhatsApp; EU/Brazil antitrust probes reopened WhatsApp access for Poke in those markets

**The OpenClaw comparison is direct:** TechCrunch frames Poke as "an OpenClaw for the rest of us" — OpenClaw requires terminal, CLI, dependency management; Poke requires nothing but a phone number. The co-founder explicitly calls out Jensen Huang saying "every company needs its own OpenClaw strategy."

**What this means for Alizé:**
- The consumer/prosumer text-first AI agent is real, funded, and growing (10x users in months)
- The OpenClaw comparison validates the personal AI agent paradigm — but also highlights the access gap
- Alizé's positioning as a managed B2B service (no CLI required from the client, human oversight, French regulatory alignment) is essentially the *enterprise* version of what Poke does for consumers
- The model-agnostic approach (routing to best model per task) is worth noting — Alizé could adopt similar multi-model routing for French PME
- EU antitrust action against Meta's WhatsApp chatbot exclusion is a potential unlock: if Meta opens WhatsApp to third-party AI agents in the EU, Poke + Alizé could have a WhatsApp channel

## 2. MCP vs Skills: The Debate on Hacker News

**Source:** [HN: "I still prefer MCP over skills" — 186 points, 157 comments](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47712718), [david.coffee blog post](https://david.coffee/i-still-prefer-mcp-over-skills/)

This is the most engaged AI toolchain discussion on HN right now. The core argument from a heavy AI user (Claude Code, Codex, ChatGPT, Perplexity daily):

**Why MCP wins for service integration:**
- Remote MCP servers require zero local install, auto-update, OAuth auth — works from any device or client
- Clean API abstraction: LLM calls `devonthink.do_x()`, server handles the rest
- Naturally sandboxed: controlled interface rather than raw execution
- Services should expose MCP endpoints, not force users to manage CLIs + raw tokens

**Why Skills win for knowledge/context:**
- Pure knowledge skills (how to format commits, internal jargon, workflow standards) work perfectly
- No CLI required — works in standard ChatGPT/Claude web interface
- Great for teaching LLM about business context, communication style, organizational structure

**The HN community consensus (rich thread, 157 comments):**
- "MCP is the best way to interact with external services *persistently* at organizational scale"
- "Skills should be called LLM_MANUAL.md, MCPs should be called Connectors"
- "For one-off API calls, use the API directly. For persistent cross-session access, MCP."
- Pushback: "MCP doesn't compose well at scale" and "the spec is unnecessarily complicated"
- Sharp insight: "LLMs default to CLIs because they're overrepresented in training data and are more inspectable by design"

**Alizé takeaway:** This directly validates Alizé's MCP-first strategy. The practitioner debate confirms what the 06h pulse found: MCP is the right integration layer for services, Skills are the right knowledge layer. Alizé should lean into being the "MCP connector specialist for French PME" — the white space between "build your own MCP setup" (technical) and "use a no-code platform" (too limited).

## 3. Enterprise AI Landscape 2026: Trust + Lock-in Framework

**Source:** [Kai Waehner — Enterprise Agentic AI Landscape 2026](https://www.kai-waehner.de/blog/2026/04/06/enterprise-agentic-ai-landscape-2026-trust-flexibility-and-vendor-lock-in/)

Updated positioning framework (covered partially in 06h pulse, key additions this cycle):

**Mistral confirmed in Trusted + Flexible quadrant:**
- Open-weight models + French jurisdiction + EU AI Act alignment
- Mistral Forge (early 2026): custom model training on enterprise's own data — reduces US hyperscaler dependency
- "The bridge between openness and production readiness that European enterprises have been looking for"

**Anthropic: High trust + manageable lock-in:**
- Accessible via direct API, AWS Bedrock, Google Vertex AI, Azure — multi-cloud reduces single-vendor risk
- EU data residency available via Bedrock
- April 1 Claude Code source leak acknowledged — "no customer data involved, no security breach"

**Apertus (ETH Zurich):**
- Open research initiative (not a commercial vendor)
- Most flexible by design — no commercial lock-in whatsoever
- September 2025 release by ETH Zurich, EPFL, Swiss National Supercomputing Centre

**Key insight for Alizé's model strategy:**
The multi-model routing that Poke uses (best model per task) is also what Kai Waehner recommends at enterprise scale. Alizé doesn't need to pick one model — it can route Mistral for EU-regulated data, Anthropic for safety-sensitive tasks, and open-source for cost-sensitive ones. The MCP layer makes this routing transparent and auditable.

## 4. AI Hallucination as Advertising Attack Vector

**Source:** [Nature, April 2026 — "Scientists invented a fake disease. AI told people it was real"](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01100-y) (37 points on HN)

Researchers invented a fake medical condition and seeded it on the internet. Major LLMs subsequently presented it as real. The discussion thread is a sharp reminder:

- "This is the future of advertising" — seeding fake information to game AI product recommendations
- "LLMs are extremely post-structuralist — they force the user to decide between plausible-looking text and verification"
- "We need trustworthy sources. For both humans and AI."

**What this means for Alizé:**
- For French PME operating in regulated industries (healthcare admin, legal, finance), AI hallucination is not just a technical problem — it's a liability
- Alizé's human oversight layer (one of its key differentiators vs pure DIY platforms) directly addresses this concern
- The EU AI Act's transparency requirements will force enterprises to document where and how AI generates content — Alizé's governance layer should capture this
- This is a selling point for Alizé's managed service vs letting employees use ungoverned AI freely
