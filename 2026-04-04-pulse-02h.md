## Signal 1: Deloitte 2026 — SaaS AI Agents Reshaping Pricing, UI, and Org Structure

Deloitte's latest SaaS + AI agents report (April 2026) makes three structural predictions that directly impact Alizé's positioning:

**Pricing shift to outcome-based models:**
Deloitte says the industry is moving from subscription toward outcome/value-based pricing for AI agents — paying based on what the agent actually produces (tickets resolved, leads qualified, invoices followed up). This is exactly what Alizé has been considering. The catch: proving agent-created value is hard when multi-agent systems span multiple vendors. **This is Alizé's governance layer opportunity** — be the attribution and audit trail that makes outcome-based pricing viable.

**The "attention layer" becomes a battleground:**
SaaS vendors want to keep users in their apps. Deloitte predicts a new category: standalone "control centers" that aggregate agents from multiple vendors — tracking usage, spend, security, compliance. Third-party agent management platforms will emerge. Alizé's governance/oversight positioning maps directly to this. French PME need someone who can manage agents across tools they already use (Sage, Pennylane, their CRM) without being locked into one vendor.

**Workers become "AI orchestrators":**
Deloitte predicts workers will spend increasing time supervising AI agents — setting goals, validating outputs, correcting errors. This is cultural, not just technical. Alizé's "pilot deployment" model (deploy → supervise → iterate) is ahead of this curve. The positioning should lean into this: we're not just selling an agent, we're selling the workflow design + supervision + governance.

## Signal 2: MIT Sloan + BCG — 35% Already Deployed, Gap Between Leaders and Laggards Widening

MIT Sloan published a spring 2025 survey (conducted with BCG) showing:
- 35% of organizations have already deployed AI agents (as of 2023, with rapid growth since)
- Another 44% have active plans to deploy
- **Leading vendors (Microsoft, Salesforce, Google, IBM) are embedding agentic AI directly in their platforms** — making agents the default interface

Key finding: even the most advanced organizations **don't fully understand how to maximize agent productivity**. The biggest gaps are in:
- Governance and risk management
- Measuring ROI
- Integrating agents across multiple vendor platforms

**What this means for Alizé:**
The adoption curve is real and accelerating. The window to position as the "French AI agent specialist for PME" is narrowing — Microsoft and Google are coming from the top down with enterprise agents, but French small businesses (5-50 employees) are underserved by these platforms. Alizé fills the gap for businesses that need agents but can't afford Microsoft Copilot deployments.

The governance gap is Alizé's specific wedge. The market is realizing agents need oversight — Alizé should make this its headline differentiator: "AI agents that work, supervised by humans, backed by audit trails."

## Signal 3: Forrester Declares — "Agents Are Now the Center of the DXP"

Forrester's Q4 2025 Digital Experience Platform wave made a sharp statement: agents are no longer a feature or add-on in enterprise platforms — they are the center of the architecture.

This is important for Alizé in two ways:

1. **Platforms are commoditizing agentic features.** As every SaaS vendor adds agents, the differentiation shifts from "has agents" to "has better agents + better orchestration + better governance." Alizé's value isn't the agent itself — it's the combination of domain-specific agent configuration, French business context, and oversight layer.

2. **Agent marketplaces will emerge.** Deloitte predicts companies will publish and discover agents dynamically, similar to app stores. Alizé could position as the French marketplace curator — "curated AI agents for French business workflows, configured and supervised by our team." This maps to the Alizé vision from day one.

## Signal 4: OpenAI Codex App — "Command Center for AI Agents" on macOS

OpenAI launched Codex App (February 2026) for macOS, positioning it explicitly as a "command center" for autonomous agents. This is a direct competitor to Anthropic's Claude Code in the developer tooling space.

**Why this matters for Alizé's tech choices:**
- Codex App is a desktop application that orchestrates AI agents locally
- This mirrors the trend toward agent control centers noted by Deloitte
- For Alizé's tech stack: the agent runtime could be local (user's infrastructure) or cloud — Codex App shows the trend toward user-controlled agent environments
- Alizé's agents should be deployable in both modes: managed cloud (for PME who want zero maintenance) and on-premise (for larger companies with data sovereignty requirements)

## Strategic Implication for Alizé — "Governance as Moat"

Synthesizing all signals this week:

The market is now in **Phase 2 of the agentic wave**: everyone has announced agents, but nobody has solved the operational reality. The gaps are:
- Multi-vendor agent coordination
- Outcome measurement and attribution
- French business context (regulations, tools, language)
- Human oversight and correction workflows

**Alizé's governance layer is the defensible moat** — not the agents themselves (which will be commoditized by Microsoft/Google within 18 months), but the layer that makes agents trustworthy, auditable, and accountable.

Concrete positioning statement: *"Alizé deploys AI agents inside your business tools — configured for French workflows, supervised by your team, with full audit trails and compliance documentation. Not another chatbot. A digital colleague that works, with a human in the loop."*
