## Signal 1: Kestra Raises $25M — Enterprise Orchestration Is the Backbone of AI at Scale

Kestra (French, open source) closed a $25M Series A led by RTP Global, with Alven, ISAI, and Axeleo participating. Total raised: $36M. The company was originally built internally at Leroy Merlin to coordinate complex automated workflows across data, infrastructure, and business operations.

**Key facts:**
- Revenue multiplied 25x in 18 months
- Executed 2B+ workflows in 2025 (20x growth year-over-year)
- Clients: JPMorgan, Toyota, Deutsche Telekom, Bloomberg, Crédit Agricole
- Open source core + cloud managed offering (Kestra 2.0 in the pipeline)
- 50 employees today, plans to double to 100
- Founded by Emmanuel Darras (CEO)

**What this means for Alizé:**
Kestra is the clearest French example of "orchestration layer" becoming a standalone business. Their pitch: as AI agents proliferate inside enterprises, something has to coordinate the workflows, ensure reliability, and provide observability. That's Kestra.

For Alizé, this is both a **signal and a warning**:
- **Signal**: Enterprises are actively buying workflow orchestration. If Alizé positions its agents as doing real operational work inside business tools, it needs an orchestration/governance layer — which is exactly what Kestra sells, but for the AI agent context.
- **Warning**: If Kestra adds agentic capabilities (which is natural given their workflow pedigree), they become a direct competitor to Alizé's governance + deployment offering.

The key differentiation for Alizé: **domain specificity and managed service for French PME**, vs Kestra's enterprise infrastructure play. Alizé should lean harder into "we deploy and operate the agents for you, not just provide the orchestration tool."

## Signal 2: Protected — €20M+ ARR, Bootstrapped, Profitable, Expanding Across Europe

Protected (French cybersecurity startup, founder Tom Wilner) disclosed over €20M ARR with no VC funding. 150,000 customers. B2B2C model via distribution partners: telecoms (SFR, Bouygues Telecom), comparators (Selectra), banks, insurers. Rule of 40 achieved. 50 employees, hiring 25% more in 2026. Goal: 1M customers by 2030.

**Key facts:**
- "Cybercare" model: aggregates NordVPN, Bitdefender, F-Secure + 24h human assistance + insurance (Groupama)
- 20,000 interventions performed (20% fraud/phishing, 33% user support, 33% hacking)
- No marketing spend — 100% partner/channel distribution
- Starting price: €6.99/month per user
- Profitable without VC — rare for B2B SaaS at this stage

**What this means for Alizé:**
Protected is the proof-of-concept for the "vertical specialist + channel distribution + profitable without VC" model that Alizé could replicate. Three lessons:

1. **Channel > direct sales**: Protected never acquired a customer directly. Every customer came through a partner. Alizé should think about distribution partners from day one — maybe system integrators (like the Atos relationships Qevlar has), or SaaS editors who could white-label Alizé agents into their products.

2. **Vertical bundling works**: Protected bundles multiple point solutions (VPN, antivirus, insurance) into one subscription. Alizé could bundle agents for a specific vertical (e.g., accounting tasks + invoicing + reporting for auto-entrepreneurs) and sell through sector-specific partners.

3. **Bootstrapping is viable**: €20M ARR without VC is rare but possible. This suggests Alizé doesn't need to raise VC to be legitimate — it can grow through revenue, which Louis has already indicated as the preferred path.

## Signal 3: Databricks State of AI Agents 2026 — 327% Growth in Multi-Agent Systems

Databricks published their 2026 State of AI Agents report (20,000+ organizations surveyed). Key findings:

- Enterprises are rapidly moving from single chatbots to **multi-agent systems** — growth of 327% in under 4 months
- **Governance and evaluation tools** are the top driver of AI project success: companies using AI governance get 12x more projects into production
- Over 80% of databases are now built by AI agents
- Most common enterprise use cases: customer service automation, code generation, data pipeline management

**What this means for Alizé:**
The 12x multiplier from governance tools is the most important data point for Alizé's positioning. The pitch isn't "our agents are smarter" — it's "our governance layer gets your AI projects to production and keeps them there." This should be the core Alizé message.

Multi-agent systems (327% growth) also validates Alizé's multi-agent architecture for each client. As French PME scale from 1 agent to 5+ agents, the coordination and governance problem becomes acute — exactly what Alizé's managed service addresses.

## Signal 4: Ludocare — First French Digital Therapy Reimbursed by HAS

Ludocare received a favorable opinion from France's Haute Autorité de Santé (HAS) for reimbursement of its digital therapy app for childhood asthma. This is the first time HAS recognized "sufficient expected service" from a mobile app in pediatric asthma.

**What this means for Alizé:**
Not a direct competitor or partner, but a signal about how French regulatory pathways work for digital products. The HAS reimbursement framework for digital health apps (PRDA with "service attendu suffisant") is now proven — this same framework could eventually apply to AI-powered operational tools in other sectors.

More importantly: Ludocare navigated the French regulatory process successfully. For Alizé, this suggests French institutions are open to new digital product categories — but the regulatory navigation takes time and expertise. Alizé's governance layer (compliance, audit trails) could eventually be positioned as helping clients meet potential future regulatory requirements.

## Immediate Actions for Alizé

1. **Kestra partnership or positioning clarity**: Kestra is building the enterprise orchestration layer. Alizé is building the agent deployment + governance layer for French PME. These are adjacent but different scale points. Alizé should either: (a) position as "Kestra for agents" in the PME segment, or (b) explore integration with Kestra for clients that need enterprise-grade orchestration.

2. **Add the "12x governance multiplier" to Alizé's pitch deck**: Databricks data makes this concrete and credible. This should be the lead slide, not a footnote.

3. **Protected's channel model is the playbook for Alizé's growth**: Don't try to sell direct to French PME. Find sector-specific distribution partners (expert-comptable firms, industry associations, SaaS editors) who already have the client relationships. Alizé becomes the agent engine behind their existing products.

4. **Domain name decision is still blocking**: alize.ai or alize.fr has been open for 3+ weeks. Louis needs to decide. This is the single biggest blocker to any brand-building.

5. **First pilot customer > anything else**: The market (funding, Databricks data, Kestra's traction) is validating the AI agent space. Alizé's priority should be getting one French PME to deploy an agent in production — that's the proof point that matters for everything else.
