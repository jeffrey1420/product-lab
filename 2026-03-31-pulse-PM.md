## Signal 1: Agentova — The Most Direct French Competitor

**Agentova is the closest French competitor to Alizé's target market.** Here's the full picture:

**What they offer:**
- Up to 8 specialized AI agents: SAV (customer service), prospecting, content creation, admin, SEO, sales
- No-code SaaS platform — connects to email, WhatsApp, Instagram, LinkedIn, Notion, calendar via API
- 24/7 availability, adapts to brand voice
- "Installs" via URL — reads site and documents to answer naturally
- Free trial: 7 days; then monthly subscription + per-minute usage (≈0.05€/min)

**Market position:**
- Targeting: entrepreneurs, TPE, small PME (same as Alizé's lower end)
- Trustpilot: 4.2/5 (43 reviews), 185+ entrepreneurs using it
- Coverage: customer service, marketing, prospecting, admin, SEO
- **Notable gap:** No governance layer, no human supervision, no enterprise reporting, no MCP connectors

**What this means for Alizé:**
Agentova is winning the "TPE/auto-entrepreneur" segment with no-code simplicity. Alizé's differentiation — governance, supervision, MCP connectors, ETI/PME enterprise-grade — is exactly what Agentova lacks. Alizé should explicitly position above Agentova: "Not just agents — operationally managed with full oversight."

## Signal 2: France AI Agents Market — $0.20B → $1.90B by 2030

**Grand View Research data confirms the French market is accelerating:**

- **2024 market size:** USD 0.20 billion
- **2030 projection:** USD 1.90 billion
- **CAGR:** 46.6% (2025-2030)
- **Key verticals:** Manufacturing, healthcare, finance, retail
- **Drivers:** Government National AI Strategy, startup investment, tech-company × research institution collaborations

**Nuance:** These figures likely include all AI agents (not just business process agents). But the trajectory validates the timing. France is not behind — it's an early mover in Europe with strong state support.

**Implication for Alizé:** The window to capture market position before US platforms (Manus/Meta, Automation Anywhere, ServiceNow) fully localize is 12-18 months. Alizé should prioritize landing 2-3 pilot clients in 2026 to establish references.

## Signal 3: French Competition Authority Opens Investigation on Conversational Agents

**The Autorité de la concurrence opened an ex officio investigation on January 9, 2026** into the conversational agents sector, followed by a public consultation launched January 29, 2026.

**Scope:**
- Definition: "generative AI-based tools that interact with users in real time in natural language, capable of responding to queries or assisting users with tasks in an automated manner"
- Geographic scope: primarily France, but with EU-wide implications
- Key questions: market definition, barriers to entry, competitive dynamics, behavior of large vertically integrated players
- Consultation deadline: March 6, 2026 (submissions now closed)

**Why this matters for Alizé:**
1. **Regulatory tailwind:** Scrutiny of big platforms (OpenAI, Google, Microsoft) creates space for independent French/EU players
2. **Market definition opportunity:** If the Authority defines "conversational agents" narrowly, it may not capture Alizé's managed service positioning — but this is also a risk if it defines broadly and creates compliance requirements
3. **Data residency and sovereignty** will likely become a competitive advantage — Alizé should emphasize French/EU data hosting

## Signal 4: Mistral AI — Platform Evolution in 2026

**Mistral AI has continued evolving from model provider toward agent infrastructure:**

- **Mistral 3** released in early 2026 — positioning as general-purpose frontier model
- **Mistral OCR** — document understanding pipeline (mobile-form-quality extraction)
- **Magistral-small-2509** (September 2025) — 24B parameter small reasoning model with image understanding
- **Codestral** — 22B code generation model

**Strategic observation:** Mistral is not yet a direct Alizé competitor — they're a model layer. But their trajectory suggests they'll move toward agent orchestration (like Manus, like AutoGPT). Alizé's MCP-first integration strategy positions it well to leverage Mistral as a model provider rather than compete with them.

**Action item:** Louis/Gabin should evaluate Mistral as a potential model backend for Alizé pilots — potentially lower cost than OpenAI/Claude for French-language workloads.

## Key Takeaways for Alizé

1. **Agentova is the primary French competitor to monitor** — their positioning is "team of AI agents, no code, fast setup." Alizé wins by going upmarket: governance, supervision, MCP, ETI-grade contracts.

2. **French market CAGR 46.6%** — timing is right. The 12-18 month window before US platforms localize is real.

3. **Autorité de la concurrence scrutiny** creates space for independent players. Alizé should monitor regulatory developments and position data sovereignty as a feature.

4. **Mistral AI as potential backend** — evaluate Mistral for French-language workloads in Alizé pilots. Lower cost, French sovereignty angle.

5. **Agentova's weaknesses are Alizé's strengths:**
   - Agentova: no governance → Alizé: full operational supervision
   - Agentova: no MCP → Alizé: MCP-first integrations
   - Agentova: TPE focus → Alizé: PME/ETI enterprise-grade
   - Agentova: no human validation → Alizé: "opérationnel avec supervision"
