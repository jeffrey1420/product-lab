## Signal 1: Claude Design — Anthropic's Full-Stack Workspace Play

Anthropic launched **Claude Design** (April 17, 2026), a visual creation tool powered by Claude Opus 4.7. It can produce interactive prototypes, slides, presentations, one-pagers, and marketing materials from prompts alone — or from uploaded DOCX/PPTX/XLSX files. The tool can ingest an entire design system (source files + code) and apply brand typography, colors, and components automatically. Multiple colleagues can collaborate in real time within the same file.

**Key capabilities:**
- Exports to Canva natively, plus PDF, PPTX, HTML
- Direct handoff to Claude Code for development from a single prompt
- Can scrape design from any live website as inspiration (not copy)
- Group conversations with Claude in the interface

**Market impact:** Figma stock dropped 7% on the announcement. This is Anthropic deliberately expanding from "AI assistant" into "AI-powered workspace" — a pattern that should worry every SaaS vertical.

**What this means for Alizé:**
Anthropic is methodically building the full Claude OS: Cowork (Jan 2026), Opus 4.7 (April 16), Design (April 17), and presumably more. The message is clear: the AI platform battle is now a workspace battle. For Alizé, this means:
1. The foundation models are good enough. The moat is now in vertical workflows and customer relationships, not model quality.
2. Claude Design eliminates another category of routine agency work (visual design, pitch decks). The pressure on web agencies to add AI services is accelerating.
3. Alizé's managed IA agents positioning becomes more relevant as the horizontal AI layer gets commoditized.

## Signal 4: Anthropic's "Building Effective Agents" — The Definitive Taxonomy

Anthropic's internal paper on building effective agents has become the de facto industry framework. Its core distinction: most "AI agents" in production are actually **workflows** (deterministic pipelines), not true agents (autonomous systems that reason and adapt). Real agents are appropriate only when:
- You can't predict the steps needed in advance
- The environment is too complex for rules
- Trusting the agent's judgment outweighs the risk of errors

**What this means for Alizé:**
This framework is Alizé's best sales material. Clients who understand the workflow/agent distinction will be better Alizé clients — they know what they're buying. Lead with this framing in pitches: "We'll determine together whether your workflow fits an agent pattern or whether a designed pipeline is more reliable and cost-effective."

The taxonomy also protects Alizé from over-promising. Don't sell "AI agents" for tasks that are actually structured workflows. This is the kind of honest positioning that builds enterprise trust.

## Signal 5: Multi-Agent Systems Replacing RPA — Deloitte Confirms the Shift

Deloitte's April 2026 research confirms what vertical AI plays like Qevlar have proven: **agentic AI-powered automation replaces RPA**, not just augments it. Key distinction:

- **RPA:** Automates structured, rules-based, repetitive tasks
- **Agentic AI:** Automates entire workflows, elevates humans to visioning, enables "self-healing" automation that corrects its own inefficiencies

The ROI case shifts from "save time on task X" to "redesign the entire process around an AI-native workflow." For Alizé clients, this means:
- A single Alizé agent replacing 3-5 RPA bots (because it handles context, exceptions, and adaptation)
- The conversation moves from "tool cost" to "process transformation value"

This is the pitch Alizé should lead with for PME decision-makers who've already tried RPA and found it brittle.
